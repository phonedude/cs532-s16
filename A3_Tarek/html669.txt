<p>&nbsp;<span style="line-height: 1.5;">The Health Behaviour Change Research Group (HBCRG)</span><span style="line-height: 1.5;">&nbsp;is a national hub and internationally-recognised centre of excellence in health behavioural intervention development, evaluation and implementation.</span><span style="line-height: 1.5;">&nbsp;</span></p>
<p>The HBCRG was established within the&nbsp;<a href="http://www.nuigalway.ie/psy/" target="_blank" title="Psychology">School of Psychology</a>&nbsp;at NUI Galway in January 2014 by Dr Molly Byrne, with support from a&nbsp;<a href="http://www.hrb.ie/home/media/press-release/?tx_ttnews%5Btt_news%5D=491&amp;tx_ttnews%5BbackPid%5D=566&amp;cHash=553a6a4d4023ce61647baf045cafec57" target="_blank" title="HRB RL Award">Health Research Board Research Leaders Award</a>.&nbsp;</p>

<h4>Core Members</h4>
<p><img src="/media/schoolofpsychology/HBCRG-web-banner-2016.png" alt="HBCRG banner 2016" style="width : 729px; height : 109px;    "  /></p>
<p><strong>&zwnj;<img src="/media/schoolofpsychology/MByrne_webbanner.jpg" alt="" style="width : 71px; height : 107px;  padding : 0px 10px 10px 0px;  float : left;"  /><a href="http://www.nuigalway.ie/our-research/people/psychology/mollybyrne/" target="_blank" title="Dr Molly Byrne">Dr Molly Byrne</a>, Senior Research Lecturer and HRB Research Leader; Director, Health Behaviour Change Research Group, School of Psychology, NUI Galway</strong></p>
<p>Molly&rsquo;s research has primarily been in developing health behaviour interventions (e.g. secondary prevention of heart disease; self-management of diabetes) within health care settings. She works with a number of multidisciplinary research teams, and is interested in the processes involved in developing behavioural interventions and testing these in real world settings. She has managed a number of randomized controlled trials of behavioural interventions and has published her work widely.</p>
<p>Molly was awarded a HRB Research Leader Award in 2013 and since January 2014 has held a full-time research leadership role at the School of Psychology, as Director of the Health Behaviour Change Research Group.</p>
<p>&zwnj;<img src="/media/schoolofpsychology/Jen_headshot_webbanner.jpg" alt="" style="width : 79px; height : 107px;  padding : 0px 10px 10px 0px;  float : left;"  /><strong><a href="http://www.nuigalway.ie/our-research/people/psychology/jennymcsharry/">Dr Jenny Mc Sharry</a>, Post-Doctoral Researcher; Assistant Director, Health Behaviour Change Research Group, School of Psychology, NUI Galway</strong></p>
<p>Jenny&rsquo;s research interests include:</p>
<ul>
<li>Interventions to change health-related behaviours and the use of behaviour change techniques.</li>
<li>Understanding and managing health in the context of multiple illnesses.</li>
<li>Illness perceptions of people with chronic conditions.</li>
<li>Adherence to medication.</li>
</ul>
<p><strong><a href="http://www.nuigalway.ie/our-research/people/psychology/valerieparker/" target="_blank" title="Valerie Parker"><img src="/media/schoolofpsychology/VParker_web.jpg" alt="" style="width : 90px; height : 107px;  padding : 0px 10px 10px 0px;  float : left;"  />Valerie Parker</a>, Strategic Development and Project Manager, Health Behaviour Change Research Group, School of Psychology, NUI Galway</strong></p>
<p>Valerie obtained an honours degree in French and History from University College Cork before coming to NUI Galway to complete postgraduate diploma in Arts Administration. After working in the arts and creative sector for 5 years, she returned to NUI Galway and graduated with a 1st Class Honours Master of Business Studies in 2005. She then worked in the Whitaker Institute for&nbsp;Innovation and Societal Change (formally the Centre for Innovation and Structural Change) as a Research Assistant, Administrative Assistant, Acting Development Officer, and from 2012-2014 was the Dissemination and Engagement Officer for the Institute. In May 2014, Valerie joined the Health Behaviour Change Research Group.</p>
<p><strong><a href="mailto:l.hynes2@nuigalway.ie"><img src="/media/schoolofpsychology/LHynes_pic2014_webbanner.jpg" alt="" style="width : 83px; height : 107px;  padding : 0px 10px 10px 0px;  float : left;"  />Ms Lisa Hynes</a>, Research Associate, Health Behaviour Change Research Group, School of Psychology, NUI Galway</strong></p>
<p>Lisa completed a degree in psychology and a Master&rsquo;s degree in health psychology at NUI, Galway. As part of a research project to improve outcomes for young adults with Type 1 diabetes, Lisa is conducting a mixed methods project to develop and test a theory of clinic attendance in order to inform the design of a complex intervention to improve attendance and engagement at young adult diabetes clinics in Ireland. She is interested in the impact of the environment and inequality in determining health, health psychology across the lifespan, and health service delivery.</p>
<p>&zwnj;<img src="/media/schoolofpsychology/PMurphy_webbanner.jpg" alt="" style="width : 94px; height : 107px;  padding : 0px 10px 10px 0px;  float : left;"  /><strong><a href="http://www.nuigalway.ie/our-research/people/psychology/murphyp/" target="_blank" title="Patrick Murphy">Dr&nbsp;</a><a href="http://www.nuigalway.ie/our-research/people/psychology/murphyp/" target="_blank" title="Patrick Murphy">Patrick Murphy</a>,&nbsp;Project Manager, CHARMS Study Research Project, School of Psychology, NUI Galway</strong></p>
<p>Patrick recently completed a PhD in Psychology at Trinity College Dublin, focusing on the disclosure of HIV seropositivity in the context of casual sex between men. He also holds a Higher Diploma in Psychology and a Postgraduate Diploma in Statistics from Trinity College, and a Bachelor of Science in Computer Science from UCC. Patrick joined the HBCRG in April 2015 on a two-year contact, as project manager for a pilot evaluation of the CHARMS intervention which aims to improve sexual assessment and counselling in hospital-based cardiac rehabilitation.</p>
<p><strong><a href="mailto:miloufredrix1@nuigalway.ie"><img src="/media/schoolofpsychology/MFredrix_web.jpg" alt="" style="width : 76px; height : 107px;  padding : 0px 10px 10px 0px;  float : left;"  />Ms Milou Fredrix</a>, PhD student, Structured PhD in Psychology and Health, School of Psychology, NUI&nbsp; Galway</strong></p>
<p>Milou completed a BSc. in Psychology and Neuroscience and a MSc in Health and Social Psychology at Maastricht University in the Netherlands. As part of her masters Milou conducted research on eating behaviour and the underlying psychological mechanisms. As a PhD student at NUI Galway, Milou will focus on &rsquo;understanding and managing health-related behaviours within the area of Diabetes&rsquo;. She is the recipient of a four-year scholarship from the Health Research Board as part of Dr Byrne's HRB Research Leaders Award.</p>
<p><strong>&zwnj;<img src="/media/schoolofpsychology/CFlannery_web.jpg" alt="" style="width : 96px; height : 107px;  padding : 0px 10px 10px 0px;  float : left;"  /><a href="mailto:cflannery@ucc.ie">Ms Caragh Flannery</a>, PhD Student and SPHeRE Scholar, School of Psychology, NUI Galway</strong></p>
<p>Caragh is a HRB SPHeRE PhD Scholar in Population Health and Health Service Research. She graduated with a Joint Bachelor&rsquo;s Degree in Economics and Geography in 2010 and with a Masters in Health Economics in 2011 from University College Cork. Her thesis entitled &ldquo;&rsquo;Some like it Hot&rsquo;: The Tan Seeking Behaviours of Irish University Students&rdquo; adopted a quantitative approach, collection of primary data, a review of factors influencing risky health behaviours including psychological theories and the application of empirical methodologies including econometric analysis.&nbsp; Previously Caragh worked in the Health Economics Research Group in the University of Surrey Guildford, on a &ldquo;Hospice at Home&rdquo; intervention which focused on patients&rsquo; preferred place of death, palliative care and rapid response teams. Furthermore Caragh worked for the Dept. General Practice in UCC conducting a systematic review on the role of the Diabetes Nurse Specialist.&nbsp; Caragh&rsquo;s research interest includes diabetes, service delivery and health behaviours.</p>
<p><strong>&zwnj;<img src="/media/schoolofpsychology/Marita2_web.jpg" alt="" style="width : 71px; height : 107px;  padding : 0px 10px 10px 0px;  float : left;"  /><a href="mailto:m.hennessy11@nuigalway.ie" target="_blank" title="Marita Hennessy">Ms&nbsp;Marita Hennessy</a>, PhD Student and SPHeRE Scholar, School of Psychology, NUI Galway</strong></p>
<p>Marita holds a BSc in Nutrition and a Diploma in Youth and Community Work from University College Cork, and a MA in Health Education and Health Promotion, and a Specialist Diploma in Teaching, Learning and Scholarship from the University of Limerick. She initially worked in the food industry for four years and since 2005 has held a number of research positions with safefood, University College Cork, the Children&rsquo;s Research Centre-Trinity College Dublin, the National Cancer Registry and Waterford Institute of Technology. Marita&rsquo;s research interests include nutrition, food poverty, obesity, health promotion, health behaviour change, children and young people, and men&rsquo;s health. As a Health Research Board-funded SPHeRE Scholar, Marita will be investigating infant feeding practices to inform a complex intervention in childhood obesity.</p>
<p>&zwnj;&zwnj;<img src="/media/schoolofpsychology/AOConnell-web-small.jpg" alt="Anthony OConnell web small" style="width : 90px; height : 105px;  padding : 10px;  float : left;"  /><strong><a href="mailto:ahjoconnell@gmail.com" target="_blank" title="Anthony O'Connell">Mr&nbsp;Anthony O'Connell</a>, Research Assistant, Health Behaviour Change Research Group, School of Psychology, NUI Galway</strong></p>
<p>Anthony completed a BA (Psychology) in NUI Galway in 2013 with first class honours, before moving to University College Cork to complete a Masters in Applied Psychology in which he also achieved first class honours. Over the course of these degrees he completed research both on the effects of salient sounds on recall and the effect of topic interest on false memory. He has presented on his undergraduate thesis at the PSI Conference in 2013 and is currently working on his Master&rsquo;s thesis for publication. He has also previously worked as an assistant psychologist with the Bessborough Centre in Cork where his duties included providing support to the centre&rsquo;s residential parent and baby unit and serving as part of a multi-disciplinary parenting capacity assessment team. During his time in the centre he also completed research into the effects of a parent and baby group on increasing parental reflective functioning and presented on this at the UNESCO Child and Family Research Centre 7th Biennial International Conference in June 2015. Currently, alongside his work with the Health Behaviour Change Research Group, he is working as an assistant psychologist with the South Galway CAMHS team and his long term goal is to gain a place on a clinical doctoral program.</p>

<h4>Affiliated Faculty</h4>
<p><a href="http://www.nuigalway.ie/our-research/people/psychology/gerrymolloy/"><strong>Dr Gerry Molloy</strong>,</a> Lecturer in Psychology, School of Psychology, NUI Galway</p>
<p>Gerry&rsquo;s research focuses on the question, 'How does the immediate social environment and one's personality influence health and illness across the lifespan?' In this work self-regulation models from behavioural sciences are used to understand how aspects of social relationships interact with individual differences to influence health and illness throughout life. This programme of work has a particular focus on treatment adherence and in particular the use of daily medications.<br /><br /><a href="http://www.nuigalway.ie/our-research/people/psychology/janewalsh/"><strong>Dr Jane Walsh</strong></a>, Lecturer in Psychology, School of Psychology, NUI Galway</p>
<p>Jane&rsquo;s research has focused mainly on developing health behaviour interventions in both a public health domain and in health care settings, with a particular focus on preventive health behaviours (e.g. screening, vaccination, exercise).</p>
<p>She has worked in several multidisciplinary teams to develop theory-based interventions in applied settings. Her work also includes interventions to reduce patient pain and distress and increase adherence to medical advice.</p>
<p>Jane has obtained funding for her research from the HRB, HSE Western Region and Mid-Western Region, and the Millennium Fund and published her research internationally in high quality journals. She is the current Director of both the MSc in Health Psychology and the Structured PhD in Psychology and Health in NUI Galway.</p>
<p><a href="http://www.nuigalway.ie/our-research/people/psychology/carolineheary/"><strong>Dr Caroline Heary</strong></a>, Lecturer in Psychology, School of Psychology, NUI, Galway</p>
<p>Caroline has a particular interest in adopting a developmental approach to the health behaviours of children and adolescents. She is involved in a number of projects on understanding and explaining patterns of health behaviours during childhood and adolescence (including food consumption, physical activity and sedentary behaviours).</p>
<p>More recently, she has also worked with Dr. Jane Walsh, Noelle Martin and Jennifer Keane (School of Psychology, NUI, Galway) on the design and evaluation of interventions to prepare children for stressful medical procedures.</p>
<p><a href="mailto:corbett@tcd.ie" style="background-color: #ffffff;"><strong>Ms Teresa Corbett</strong></a>, PhD student, Structured PhD in Psychology and Health, School of Psychology, NUI Galway</p>
<p>Teresa Corbett was an undergraduate student of Psychology in Trinity College Dublin until 2011 and obtained a Masters degree in Health Psychology from NUI, Galway in 2012. She is currently a PhD student in the Structured PhD in Psychology and Health, at NUI Galway. Her research relates to the potential for psychological therapies to be used to help cancer survivors suffering with cancer-related fatigue, with a focus on e-Health interventions. She also works as a research assistant for On The Road Again, which is an organisation that promotes the self-sufficiency of homeless populations and people suffering mental health issues by engaging them in walking and running as a means to build confidence, strength and self-esteem. Teresa is also a co-editor for the European Health Psychology Society Bulletin.</p>
<p><a href="dmjwalshgmail.com"><strong>Ms. Deirdre Walsh</strong></a>, PhD student, Structured PhD in Psychology and Health, School of Psychology, NUI Galway</p>
<p>Deirdre&rsquo;s research focuses on post traumatic growth and cancer survivorship. This work addresses questions such as &rsquo;how does a physical or health related trauma facilitate positive changes in individuals in areas such as diet, exercise behaviours and well-being?&rsquo; The focus of Deirdre&rsquo;s current research is the development and psychometric evaluation of a scale to measure physical post traumatic growth in prostate cancer survivors. This research aims to broaden understanding of changes in health behaviours following trauma and cancer survivorship.</p>
<p><a href="mailto:L.RODRIGUEZ2@nuigalway.ie"><strong>Ms. Leonor Rodriguez</strong>,</a> PhD Student, Structured PhD in Child and Youth Research. School of Psychology and Child and Family Research Centre, NUI Galway</p>
<p>Leonor&rsquo;s research examines the relationship between perceived social support and adjustment of adolescent children of mothers who have recently been diagnosed with breast cancer. It also explores if this relationship is moderated and mediated by coping styles, perceived stress levels, self-efficacy and the mother-adolescent relationship. The study will also test testing the efficacy of a web based intervention to improve perceived social support and the process of adjustment in adolescents providing adolescents with skills like stress management, assertive communication, social skills, social support, help seeking and anger management.</p>
<p><a href="mailto:rojconway@gmail.com"><strong>Mr Ronan Conway</strong></a>, PhD student, Structured PhD in Child and Youth Research, School of Psychology, NUI Galway</p>
<p>Ronan&rsquo;s research focuses on the question, 'What is a &ldquo;good&rdquo; teenager, and how does individual and contextual factors influence the positive healthy development of young people?' In this work, previous theory and research on adolescent self-regulation is critiqued and expanded to promote understanding of how adolescents interact with their context to influence Positive Youth Development and risk-taking. The goal of this research is to illustrate the normative development of positive and negative indices of adolescent behaviour, in order to inform future behavioural intervention and youth promotion programmes.</p>

<h5>Monday 11 April 2016, School of Psychology, NUI Galway.</h5>
<p><strong>SOLD OUT! Please email&nbsp;<a href="mailto:valerie.parker@nuigalway.ie" target="_blank">valerie.parker@nuigalway.ie</a>&nbsp;to put your name on the waiting list. <p><a href="https://www.eventbrite.ie/e/one-day-workshop-designing-effective-interventions-for-health-behaviour-change-an-introduction-tickets-21030889958" target="_blank" title="HBCRG Workshop April 2016"><img src="/media/schoolofpsychology/HBCRG_workshop_Apr16_sm.png" alt="HBCRG workshop Apr16" style="width : 672px; height : 504px;    "  /></a>&zwnj;</p>
<p><strong>For more information, please <a href="https://www.eventbrite.ie/e/one-day-workshop-designing-effective-interventions-for-health-behaviour-change-an-introduction-tickets-21030889958" target="_blank" title="HBCRG Workshop April 2016">CLICK HERE</a> or visit <a href="http://www.tinyurl.com/HBCRG16" target="_blank" title="HBCRG Workshop April 2016">www.tinyurl.com/HBCRG16</a>&nbsp;</strong></p>
<p><strong>Workshop Details:</strong></p>
<p>Many interventions which seek to promote health are dependent on effectively changing people&rsquo;s behaviour. Behavioural science has made significant advances within the last decade in developing methodologies for intervention designers. In this workshop, participants will have an opportunity to learn about, and practice using, emerging methods for designing and evaluating behavioural interventions.</p>
<p>&nbsp;On completion of the workshop, participants will be able to:</p>
<ul>
<li>Apply a structured approach to using behavioural theory for intervention development</li>
<li>Use the COM-B (capability, opportunity, motivation &ndash; behaviour) model and the Behaviour Change Wheel to design interventions</li>
<li>Specify and describe intervention content using Behaviour Change Technique taxonomies</li>
<li>Test the theoretical underpinnings of interventions</li>
<li>Understand processes of change as part of intervention evaluation</li>
</ul>
<p>The workshop will be facilitated by: <a href="http://www.nuigalway.ie/our-research/people/psychology/mollybyrne/" target="_blank" title="Molly Byrne"><strong>Dr Molly Byrne</strong></a>, Health Research Board (HRB) Research Leader and Director of the Health Behaviour Change Research Group at NUI Galway, and <a href="http://www.nuigalway.ie/our-research/people/psychology/jennymcsharry/" target="_blank" title="Jenny Mc Sharry"><strong>Dr Jenny Mc Sharry</strong></a>, Postdoctoral Researcher, Health Behaviour Change Research Group at NUI Galway, and Registered Health Psychologist with the UK Health and Care Professions Council.</p>
<p>This workshop is for researchers, practitioners, policy makers and students interested in behaviour change, and is supported by the Health Research Board, and in association with the Health Research Board - Trials Methodology Research Network.</p>
