    <p>All that time on the shelf did little to soothe the rivalry between UFC bantamweight champion <a class="sbn-auto-link" href="http://www.mmafighting.com/fighter/328/dominick-cruz">Dominick Cruz</a> and <a class="sbn-auto-link" href="http://www.mmafighting.com/fighter/2/urijah-faber">Urijah Faber</a>.</p>
<p>The two fighters have been chirping at each other since Cruz recaptured his title with a split decision win over <a class="sbn-auto-link" href="http://www.mmafighting.com/fighter/1257/t-j-dillashaw">T.J. Dillashaw</a> in January. That became most apparent last week, when Cruz threw several shots Faber's way during Super Bowl 50 media day.</p>

<p>Cruz jabbed Faber over the recent exodus from Team Alpha Male -- one which started with Dillashaw, but has now seen <a class="sbn-auto-link" href="http://www.mmafighting.com/fighter/285/joseph-benavidez">Joseph Benavidez</a> and <a class="sbn-auto-link" href="http://www.mmafighting.com/fighter/1539/lance-palmer">Lance Palmer</a> split time with Team Elevation as well -- and said he would be willing to gift Faber a title shot since it was all Faber had left.</p>
<p>Cruz and Faber fought twice before. Faber won the first meeting in 2007 via guillotine choke to claim the WEC featherweight title, while Cruz won the 2011 rematch via unanimous decision to retain the UFC bantamweight title. The two rivals were scheduled to settle their trilogy with a rubber match in 2012, however Cruz pulled out of the bout while filming <i>The Ultimate Fighter 15</i> after suffering a torn ACL.</p>
<p>Reoccurring injuries kept Cruz largely sidelined until his win over Dillashaw, but now that the division is back to the way it once was, Faber is ready to make up for lost time. "The California Kid" took to&nbsp;<a target="_blank" href="https://www.instagram.com/p/BBqCZ7as4o-/">Instagram</a> on Thursday, responding to Cruz's challenge and proclaiming that he would be the one "to break the glass champ."</p>
