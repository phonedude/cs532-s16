<div class="field-body">
            <p dir="ltr"><span id="docs-internal-guid-82a90ec4-d175-51fe-88c0-49dc67815981">Professional racial agitator Al Sharpton seemed to challenge Senator Bernie Sanders to be more overt in his demagoguery around “racial justice.” Joining MSNBC’s Morning Joe on Thursday, Sharpton said that Sanders was insufficiently direct in addressing the “race element” as it pertains to “income inequality.”</span></p>



<p dir="ltr"><span id="docs-internal-guid-82a90ec4-d175-51fe-88c0-49dc67815981">Recalling his breakfast with Sanders on Wednesday, Sharpton recalled their discussion.</span></p>



<p dir="ltr"><span id="docs-internal-guid-82a90ec4-d175-51fe-88c0-49dc67815981">“We gotta deal with income inequality, and wages, yeah fine, but what about the race element of that?” said Sharpton, “There’s racism involved.”</span></p>



<p dir="ltr"><span id="docs-internal-guid-82a90ec4-d175-51fe-88c0-49dc67815981">“What did [Sanders] tell you?” asked Joe Scarborough.</span></p>



<p dir="ltr"><span id="docs-internal-guid-82a90ec4-d175-51fe-88c0-49dc67815981">“He did not address that directly, that’s what I was pressing him on. Are you gonna talk about affirmative action? are you gonna talk about racial disparities in terms of promotions and access to capital? And these are the things to discuss with him,” replied Sharpton.</span></p>



<p dir="ltr"><span id="docs-internal-guid-82a90ec4-d175-51fe-88c0-49dc67815981">Sharpton will be meeting with Hillary Clinton next Tuesday.</span></p>



<p dir="ltr"><span id="docs-internal-guid-82a90ec4-d175-51fe-88c0-49dc67815981">Declaring that the Democratic presidential candidates cannot take “the black vote” for granted in competition with one another, Sharpton said, “the vote is earned.”</span></p>



<p dir="ltr"><span id="docs-internal-guid-82a90ec4-d175-51fe-88c0-49dc67815981">Both Clinton and Sanders </span><a href="http://www.dailywire.com/news/2154/blacklivesmatter-clinton-and-sanders-trash-america-robert-kraychik">have expressed support</a> for anti-American racial agitation movements such as Black Lives Matter. Hyping the narrative framing blacks as an oppressed class of people in America, both presidential hopefuls seek to benefit from the left-wing cultivation of perceptions of grievance among blacks.</p>



<p dir="ltr"><span id="docs-internal-guid-82a90ec4-d175-51fe-88c0-49dc67815981">Left-wing media has framed black Democrats in the south as a “firewall” for Clinton, seemingly admitting that Sanders’s Brooklyn accent and Jewish ethnicity will be off-putting to this crucial demographic of Democratic support.</span></p>



<p dir="ltr"><span id="docs-internal-guid-82a90ec4-d175-51fe-88c0-49dc67815981">Attempting to outflank one another on the angle of racial grievance-mongering, both Clinton and Sanders have </span><a href="http://www.dailywire.com/news/2002/democrats-endorse-blacklivesmatter-chase-phantoms-robert-kraychik">chased phantoms</a> of “systemic racism” and “white privilege.”</p>
