                                <div class="subscriber-preview"><p>FAIRBANKS  There are backyard ice rinks all over Fairbanks and some of them are pretty amazing.</p></div><div class="subscriber-preview"><p>I know this because I was a judge last year for Hockey Weeks Backyard Rink Contest and the entrants were very impressive. Whether the space was big or small, the space was used effectively and often in a unique way. And the rinks were all used for the love of hockey.</p></div><div class="subscriber-only"><p>Its time once again for this popular contest, sponsored by the Fairbanks Hockey Hall of Fame.</p></div><div class="subscriber-only"><p>Its one of my favorite things about Hockey Week, because it involves the entire family and the kids are so excited, said Randy Zarnke, president of Fairbanks Hockey Hall of Fame. The enthusiasm is infectious and I get excited too.</p></div><div class="subscriber-only"><p>Hockey Week runs from Feb. 26 through March 6. The Backyard Rink Contest judging takes place from Feb. 29 through March 2. The winner will likely be announced on March 3.</p></div><div class="subscriber-only"><p>Generally, about six backyard rinks, and sometimes as many as 10, enter the contest.</p></div><div class="subscriber-only"><p>There are no hard and fast rules on the judging, Zarnke said. One of the most important things to me is seeing how many kids use it to have fun and learn the game.</p></div><div class="subscriber-only"><p>The 2015 contest was won by the Gene Sager family, of Hamilton Acres. This rink, as I recall, featured outdoor-theater-style seating and candy cane bins for hockey sticks, all in a fenced-in yard. It was hockey player heaven for kids.</p></div><div class="subscriber-only"><p>We always have a great time with our rink, Sager recently told Zarnke. All of our friends and neighbors bring their kids over to skate. Its a lot of work, but well worth the effort. I encourage everyone with kids to put a rink in their yard.</p></div><div class="subscriber-only"><p>Good luck to those families who enter the contest this year, he added.</p></div><div class="subscriber-only"><p>Hockey Week is slated at a little later time frame than usual this year and Zarnke hopes this new timing will encourage more families to put their rinks on display.</p></div><div class="subscriber-only"><p>It isnt always the big fancy rink with boards and lights that wins, Zarnke said. The small ones that get a lot of use are always my favorites.</p></div><div class="subscriber-only"><p>Holaday-Parks sponsors the winning prize, a trophy featuring the silhouette of the annual Hockey Week logo. Zarnke called it a work of art that will forever remind the winners of their rink. It can be displayed inside or somewhere out by the rink.</p></div><div class="subscriber-only"><p>To enter your backyard rink in the contest, contact Zarnke at 452-6857 or email him at <a href="mailto:itrap2@gci.net">itrap2@gci.net</a>.</p></div><div class="subscriber-only"><p><strong>Donating blood</strong></p></div><div class="subscriber-only"><p>Another part of Hockey Week I fully support is the effort to recruit donors for the Alaska Blood Bank.</p></div><div class="subscriber-only"><p>Zarnke asked me to volunteer last year, which I did. Now, he has challenged me to bring new donors with me this year. Id love to go with a mob of readers. Contact me if youre interested. Thanks to the readers who already reached me. We will coordinate our rendezvous soon.</p></div><div class="subscriber-only"><p><strong>Ladies Night</strong></p></div><div class="subscriber-only"><p>Heres another good cause to support.</p></div><div class="subscriber-only"><p>The Breast Cancer Detection Center is hosting a Ladies Night at Sportsmans Warehouse at 6 p.m. tonight. The first 100 women who arrive receive a complimentary gift bag. There will be games, prizes, a silent auction and even a wall of guns raffle to benefit BCDC.</p></div><div class="subscriber-only"><p>This special evening is an ongoing partnership between the business and the nonprofit organization. Apparently, theres a little friendly competition between the Fairbanks store and the Wasilla store, which hosts a similar night.</p></div><div class="subscriber-only"><p>Im pretty sure Fairbanks can come on top in that contest, especially since its all for a good cause.</p></div><div class="subscriber-only"><p><em>Contact community editor and columnist Kris Capps at <a href="mailto:kcapps@newsminer.com">kcapps@newsminer.com</a>, in the office at 459-7546 or by cell at 322-6334. Follow her on Twitter: @FDNMKris.</em></p></div>
