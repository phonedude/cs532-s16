                    <h2 class="mod-article-intro">‘Play Your Part for a Better Internet’ is the strapline for Safer Internet Day 2016, which we're backing today (Tuesday) to help promote the safe, responsible and positive use of digital technology for young people.</h2>
                    <p><em><a href="http://www.thefa.com/football-rules-governance/safeguarding/raising-awareness---downloads-section" target="_blank"><em>Click here to read The FA's Best Practice Guidelines</em></a>.</em><br />
<br />
Safer Internet Day is celebrated in 100+ countries, with a website at&nbsp;<a href="http://www.saferinternet.org.uk" target="_blank"><strong>www.saferinternet.org.uk</strong></a> providing resources for children, schools and families. The day offers the opportunity to highlight positive uses of technology and to explore the role everyone plays in helping to create a better and safer online community. It calls upon young people, parents, carers, teachers, social workers, law enforcement, companies and policy makers to cooperate.<br />
<br />
It is coordinated in the UK by the UK Safer Internet Centre, a partnership of three leading charities; Childnet, the South-West Grid for Learning and the Internet Watch Foundation. The joint Insafe/INHOPE network are also involved, with the support of the European Commission and 31 national Safer Internet Centres across Europe.<br />
<br />
The Essex County FA are reminding all those involved within football to interact online responsibly and to promote the values of The FA&rsquo;s Respect Programme, both on the field of play and online. The day offers the opportunity to highlight The FA&rsquo;s best practice guidance in relation to social media and explore the role everyone plays in helping to create a safe online environment within football.<br />
<br />
County Welfare Officer, Helen Hever, explained: &ldquo;A lot of young people in the UK do much of their socialising within football teams, so this is an important day in the calendar. It&rsquo;s crucial for us to offer support if they have concerns and to play our part in educating them in the positive ways of using the Internet.&rdquo;<br />
<br />
Leagues, clubs and coaches can play their part by adding links to support outlets and advice on web pages, Facebook and Twitter. They can also follow The FAs best practice guidance to ensure clubs and leagues manage online environments and communications, properly guaranteeing they are safe for all involved.<br />
<br />
Players can be kind and respectful to others online and seeking positive and safe opportunities to create, engage and share online. Parents and carers can maintain an open and honest dialogue with their children about their online lives by modelling positive online behaviours themselves, and also by reporting any inappropriate or illegal content they find.<br />
<br />
