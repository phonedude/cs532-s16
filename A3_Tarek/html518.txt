Email</span></span></span></span></a></div></div><a id="dd_start"></a><p><strong>On December 12th 2015 Conor McGregor did exactly what he claimed he would accomplish when he was first signed to the UFC in 2013&#8230; </strong></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Now the self-proclaimed Mystic Mac is set on making history by becoming the first dual-weight champion in the UFC. The man who stands in his path is the imposing, experienced Brazilian standout Rafael Dos Anjos, whose pressure fighting style, well rounded arsenal and breadth of experience has led to him becoming one of the most feared and dangerous fighters in any division.</span><strong><strong> </strong></strong></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">The 31 year-old Brazilian didn’t show the promise of a champion at the beginning of his UFC career, going 0-2 against Jeremy Stephens and Tyson Griffin between 2008 and 2009. Dos Anjos has now amassed a record of 11-1 since moving to Kings MMA in 2012 and started training under the tutelage of the legendary trainer Rafael Codeiro.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;"> Dos Anjos’ charge towards the title began with a TKO over Jason High in the June of 2014, which he followed up only 2 months later with a KO of Benson Henderson becoming the only man to ever knock out the former lightweight champion. </span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Dos Anjos then left no doubt that he was to be the next in line for a shot at the title after a mauling of Nate Diaz on a UFC Fox Show in December of 2014. Dos Anjos punished Diaz with absolutely brutal leg kicks and vicious ground and pound leaving the former title challenger cut, bruised and battered as Dos Anjos claimed the unanimous decision victory and his first title shot in the UFC after his 17</span><span style="font-weight: 400;">th</span><span style="font-weight: 400;"> fight with the company.</span></p>
<p>Before Dos Anjos’ title fight in March of 2015 at UFC 185, his opponent, the then champion, Anthony Pettis was being praised by the media and the masses as one of the best pound for pound fighters on the planet. He was set for superstardom, and 2015 was to be his year where he claimed he would repeatedly defend his title and lay waste to all contenders that stood in his way.</p>
<p>Dos Anjos was viewed as the first sacrificial lamb to be brought to the slaughter, however Dos Anjos was not reading from the same script as the rest of the world. Dos Anjos would absolutely annihilate Pettis, putting on one of the most one-sided beatings in UFC title-fight history, smashing Pettis from pillar to post for the full 5 rounds.</p>
<p>Dos Anjos beat Anthony Pettis in every single facet of martial arts, on the feet, on the ground and in the clinch. He broke Pettis mentally and physically leaving him with a broken orbital bone and without his title. It was on that night that Dos Anjos rewrote the history of the Lightweight Division, and announced his arrival as one of the greatest fighters in the sport, 6 years after his UFC debut.</p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">In his latest outing, Dos Anjos would need just a little over a minute to blast his way through his first title challenger Donald Cerrone. Cerrone had been riding an 8 fight winning streak heading into the end of year title fight with Dos Anjos on 19 December 2015, just one week after McGregor claimed the featherweight division as his own. </span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Dos Anjos would hurt Cerrone early and often with body shots before swarming the contender with punches until the referee had seen enough at 1:06 into the very first round. Dos Anjos got on the mic after the fight ended sending a clear message to one man; </span></p>
<blockquote><p><span style="font-weight: 400;">“Hey Mr McGregor, if you want to come to Lightweight division, this is my division man. You’d better stay in the featherweight division. I will fight you in Brazil, I will go to Ireland and face you there, wherever you want man. I am here to stay.”</span></p></blockquote>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">One thing is certain about this fight between Dos Anjos and McGregor, it will be the toughest test of McGregor’s career to this point. Dos Anjos is big, well rounded, in great condition and most importantly, he certainly does not fear McGregor and will not shy away from the challenge. </span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">How the fight will play out is open to interpretation, but one thing is for certain, if UFC 185 should have taught us anything, that is do not sleep on Rafael Dos Anjos, because in his own heart and mind, he is here to stay.</span></p>
