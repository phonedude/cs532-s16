<div class="article-body-content"><p class="">The City of Cleveland wants the estate of Tamir Rice &mdash; the 12-year-old, unarmed black boy <a href="http://mic.com/articles/104922/cleveland-police-shot-and-killed-a-12-year-old-boy-for-carrying-a-pellet-gun#.W5x8TlOUK">fatally shot</a> by a police officer in November 2014 &mdash; to pay for his ambulance bill.</p>
<p class="">On Wednesday an assistant law director, on behalf of the city, filed a creditor's claim against the Rice estate for the outstanding EMS bill&nbsp;in Cuyahoga County Probate Court, according to&nbsp;<a href="http://www.wkyc.com/news/local/cleveland/cleveland-wants-tamir-rice-estate-to-pay-for-his-last-dying-expense/39635629">WKYC</a>.</p>
<p class="">The shooting occurred, Officer Timothy Loehmann&nbsp;alleged, when Rice was reaching for something around his waistband, prompting Loehmann to react by&nbsp;<a href="http://www.nbcnews.com/news/us-news/cleveland-wants-tamir-rice-estate-pay-500-ambulance-bill-n516281?cid=sm_tw&amp;hootPostID=be29e830862c25288456fcc18d5d683e">shooting </a>the boy in the torso. It was later&nbsp;<a href="http://mic.com/articles/129846/disturbing-revelation-about-tamir-rice-shooting-found-in-new-video-analysis-results#.PdLYY0TvJ">revealed</a> the boy had a toy pellet gun on his person, but&nbsp;his hands were in his pockets when he was shot.</p>
<figure contenteditable="false" class="embeddable clearfix image article-image-container article-image-container-horizontal photo embeddable-image" id="embeddable-77eg1ag" data-type="image"><div class="embeddable-container"><img src="http://thumbs.mic.com/MjFjMDk3NjdkZSMvZmxtbFE2THJ4TXU4MXdIbXdLZzhSTWU4ZExVPS82MXgyMzQ6MjEwN3gyMjU2LzkwMHg4OTAvZmlsdGVyczpxdWFsaXR5KDcwKS9odHRwOi8vczMuYW1hem9uYXdzLmNvbS9wb2xpY3ltaWMtaW1hZ2VzL2lodm1uaWluZmYxeGdidGU0a3hkbmhyc2l3Z2tjOWx6cGl4ZXltYnp1bGJha2Rlc3dnc2V4Z2l4bXhobnVoZ3EuanBn.jpg" alt="Cleveland Just Billed the Estate of Tamir Rice for the Slain Boy's Ambulance Costs"></div><figcaption class="article-embeddable-attribution">Source:&nbsp;<a href="http://ap.org" class="article-embeddable-source">Tony Dejak/AP</a></figcaption></figure>
<p class="">Rice was rushed to the hospital in an ambulance where he died the following morning. And that's the ride for which the city is asking Rice's family to shell out.</p>
