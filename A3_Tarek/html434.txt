class="byline"> by <span

class="clearfix"></div><div
class="entry-content" itemprop="articleBody"><p>Hello, I hope you are doing well. In some last articles, all we talked about ideas. If you have not gone through that, Please make an effort to read <a
href="http://goentrepreneur.net/validate-your-business-idea-entrepreneurship/" target="_blank">How to Know If Your Idea Works?</a> Until you don&#8217;t have a good idea, even a good looking and professional logo can&#8217;t help you. First thing to worry about is idea, then comes a good looking logo. When i say &#8220;A Good Logo&#8221;, I think about Apple. They had spent almost half of their first income on designing a perfect logo.</p><h1><span
style="font-family: 'Comic Sans MS', sans-serif; font-size: 18pt; color: #808000;">What does a Good logo say?</span></h1><p>Logos are a critical aspect of business marketing. As the company&#8217;s major graphical representation, a logo anchors a company&#8217;s brand and becomes the single most visible manifestation of the company within the target market. For this reason, a well-designed logo is an essential part of any company&#8217;s overall marketing strategy.</p><p>A good logo appears on all of the company letterhead, communications, marketing and advertising pieces of a business as a graphic representation or symbol for the company. Essentially, the logo is typically the first thing a reader may notice before reading the text. Logos help in the creation of a brand identity for a company or business because the primary functions of a logo are to inspire trust, recognition and admiration for a business or product. A logo must be simple, memorable, timeless, appropriate and versatile, but at the same time conjure up the feeling you want your target audience to associate with your business.</p><p><span
style="color: #993300; font-size: 14pt; font-family: 'Comic Sans MS', sans-serif;">Purpose</span></p><p>Corporate logos are intended to be the &#8220;face&#8221; of a company: They are graphical displays of a company&#8217;s unique identity, and through colors and fonts and images they provide essential information about a company that allows customers to identify with the company&#8217;s core brand. Logos are also a shorthand way of referring to the company in advertising and marketing materials; they also provide an anchor point for the various fonts, colors and design choices in all other business marketing materials.</p><p><span
style="font-size: 14pt; color: #993300; font-family: 'Comic Sans MS', sans-serif;">Design Principles</span></p><p>Good logos should be unique and comprehensible to potential customers. Although there are myriad choices for color, visual elements and typography, in general a logo should help convey some information about the company, or be designed in a way that gives some sense of meaning about the company or its industry. For example, cutting-edge firms and tech companies tend to have angular logos to convey speed, while service-oriented firms have rounded logos to provide a sense of service and trust.</p><p><span
class='embed-youtube' style='text-align:center; display: block;'><iframe
class='youtube-player' type='text/html' width='676' height='411' src='http://www.youtube.com/embed/wyP8gTIXdBo?version=3&#038;rel=1&#038;fs=1&#038;autohide=2&#038;showsearch=0&#038;showinfo=1&#038;iv_load_policy=1&#038;wmode=transparent' frameborder='0' allowfullscreen='true'></iframe></span></p><p><span
style="font-family: 'Comic Sans MS', sans-serif; font-size: 14pt; color: #993300;">Brand Identity</span></p><p>Logos are the chief visual component of a company&#8217;s overall brand identity. The logo appears on stationery, websites, business cards and advertising. For that reason, a well-designed logo can contribute to business success, while a substandard logo can imply amateurishness and turn off potential customers. However, a logo should cohere well with other aspects of a company&#8217;s visual presentation: No logo, however well designed, can look good when surrounded by contradictory graphical elements or inconsistent fonts. This is why a logo is the basic unit of a larger brand identity that includes company fonts, colors and document-design guidelines.</p><p><span
style="font-family: 'Comic Sans MS', sans-serif; font-size: 14pt; color: #993300;">Return on Investment</span></p><p>As consumers grow to know, like and trust a specific brand, they are more likely to respond positively to successive encounters with a logo&#8211;potentially leading to increased sales or improved mind share within the target market. In addition, a well-designed logo implies a degree of professionalism and competence that could help steer potential new clients toward selecting the business rather than a competitor with no or substandard logos.</p><p><span
style="font-family: 'Comic Sans MS', sans-serif; font-size: 14pt; color: #993300;">Common Mistakes</span></p><p>Small businesses often play it fast-and-loose with logos, paying insufficient attention to their proper size and positioning and surrounding them with materials&#8211;including <a
href="http://www.clipart.com/" target="_blank">clipart</a>&#8211;that compete with them visually. Avoid re-creating different types of logos for specific purposes (e.g., letterhead and business cards) or having similar-but-not-identical versions for print and online purposes.</p><p><span
style="font-family: 'Comic Sans MS', sans-serif; font-size: 18pt; color: #000000;">Design it Today</span></p><p>There are several Online Logo Makers available. <a
href="http://logogarden.com" target="_blank">logogarden.com</a>, <a
href="http://logotypemaker.com" target="_blank">logotypemaker</a>, <a
href="http://designmantic.com" target="_blank">designmantic.com</a> are some of the Logo makers. So if you don&#8217;t have a good logo for your business, Make it today. Hope you enjoyed it. Let me know your opinion below.</p><p>&nbsp;</p><h3 class='coments-title'>Comments</h3><p
