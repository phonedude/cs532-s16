The proposal represents a tax increase of less than one cent ($0.004), which translates to about $8 per year on a home valued at $200,000.</p><p>Of the five categories represented  parks and recreation, public safety, public art, public facilities and streets and drainage  parks and recreation represents the largest chunk at $27 million, followed by public safety projects totaling about $24.4 million.</p><div class="tnt-ads-container text-center hidden-sm hidden-md hidden-lg"><div class="tnt-ads oas-ad" data-oas-slot="Right1" id="fixed-big-ad-top-mobile" data-oas-size="[300,250]"></div>
    </div><div class="tnt-ads-container text-center hidden-xs"><div class="tnt-ads oas-ad" data-oas-slot="Left1" id="fixed-big-ad-top-asset" data-oas-size="[300,250]"></div>
    </div><p>Following closely are propositions for street improvements ($23.89 million) and library improvements and expansion ($16 million). About 2 percent of the bond package  $1.77 million  would be dedicated to public art projects.</p><p>If approved, the bond money would help fund construction of the Rowlett Creek Park/Recreation Center; construction of Fire Station 6, to be built near Sam Rayburn Tollway (SRT); the completion of phase II of the Watters Branch Community Park; a firearms training center for Allen police; and the extension of Ridgeview Drive to U.S. Highway 75.</p><p>Joe Farmer, former mayor and chair of the steering committee that oversaw this years bond proposal, previously called this years election the largest bond proposition in total that Allen has ever engaged in.</p><p>Mayor Pro Tem Gary Caplinger said the bond election is part of Allens growing process. He added he is particularly in favor of the proposed firearms training facility that would eliminate the need for Allen PD to make a two-hour round trip to Greenville to use their facilities.</p><p>Officials previously broached the possibility of opening up the gun range for public use.</p><div class="tnt-ads-container text-center hidden-sm hidden-md hidden-lg"><div class="tnt-ads oas-ad" data-oas-slot="Right2" id="fixed-big-ad-middle-mobile" data-oas-size="[300,250]"></div>
    </div><p>Weve been very successful in public-private partnerships with other things throughout the community, so this is an opportunity, Caplinger said. At the end of the day, we need to do whats right to train our law enforcement. That would be our main focus and goal.</p><p>Chief Financial Officer Eric Cannon called the citys approach to the election very conservative.</p><p>We have a very strong financial history, he said. I think we took everything that we learned previously and took that into account in this upcoming program. I think itll be extremely successful.</p><div class="tnt-ads-container text-center hidden-sm hidden-md hidden-lg"><div class="tnt-ads oas-ad" data-oas-slot="Right3" id="fixed-big-ad-bottom-mobile" data-oas-size="[300,250]"></div>
