      <div class="m-entry__body" id="article-body">
    <p><i>The 2016 NFL offseason has begun for the <a data-ref-index="6" class="sbn-auto-link" href="http://www.sbnation.com/nfl/teams/philadelphia-eagles">Philadelphia Eagles</a>,          which means Howie Roseman and new head coach Doug Pederson will       spend   the next couple of  weeks evaluating the 2015 roster. The   team     went  7-9  last season and improvement is clearly needed. By   the  time    NFL free    agency starts on March 9, the Eagles will have a    good idea    of which    players they'll want to bring back for the   2016  season.    Today we'll  continue this offseason review series by    looking at the nose tackle position.</i></p>
<h4>The Players</h4>
<p><b>BENNIE LOGAN</b></p>
<p>Numbers: 598 snaps, 1 sack, 1 hit, 11 hurries, 39 tackles</p>
<div class="m-ad m-ad__article-body">
  
  <div id="div-gpt-ad-mobile_article_body" class="dfp_ad" data-cb-ad-id="Mobile article body" data-cb-dfp-id="unit=mobile_article_body">

<p>Review: Remember when some thought Logan was too small to play nose tackle? He disproved that once again in 2015 by having a beastly season. Logan finished finished in tackles among 3-4 nose tackles and 4-3 defensive tackles. He ranked second overall in "Run Stop Percentage," which is a Pro Football Focus signature stat that essentially tries to gauge meaningful tackles. So not just tackles where an offensive player runs through a defense and a guy finally brings him down, but instead a tackle where the defender made a good play. In other words, Logan was a run-stuffing force.</p>

<p>Logan's value to the team was apparent. Philadelphia's run defense was the best in the league early in the season before<a target="_blank" href="http://www.phillyvoice.com/eagles-run-defense-after-five-games-and-now/"> Logan got hurt and it completely fell apart</a>. Many attribute the Eagles' defensive decline to the <a target="_blank" href="http://www.bleedinggreennation.com/2015/12/5/9851284/philadelphia-eagles-defense-high-tempo-defense-offense-bill-davis-chip-kelly">amount of snaps played </a>and the loss of impressive rookie Jordan Hicks. Those were definitely significant factors, but so was losing Logan.</p>
<p>In the Eagles' old 3-4 two-gap scheme, Logan was required to read and react. He was very good at doing that, but it didn't free him up to make plays as a pass rusher. In Philadelphia's new 4-3 scheme, Logan will have the chance to attack. The combination of him and Cox as 4-3 defensive tackles should make for a very strong interior defensive line.</p>
<p><b>BEAU ALLEN</b></p>
<p>Numbers: 349 snaps, 18 tackles, 2 pass deflections</p>
<p>Review: Allen was the primary backup to Logan. He didn't really stand out in a positive way last season. As I mentioned earlier, the run defense declined when he was in and Logan was out. He might be a fine backup player but he didn't show enough to be a good starter.</p>
<h4>Who Could Leave</h4>
<p>Logan had never played in a 3-4 before joining the Eagles in 2013. Allen, meanwhile, played in a 4-3 defense three out of his four seasons with Wisconsin. One would think these players should be able to handle Philadelphia's scheme change.</p>
<h4>Who Could Sign</h4>
<p>The Eagles' starting defensive tackles will likely be Logan and Fletcher Cox. The depth behind them could be light so they'll need to fill out the roster at that spot. <a target="_blank" href="http://www.bleedinggreennation.com/2016/2/10/10952948/eagles-fletcher-cox-money-contract-defensive-end-tackle-4-3-scheme-jim-schwartz-position-review?_ga=1.242420930.1523330522.1409528280">Nick Fairley has already been suggested</a>. This is a spot where the Eagles could look to add a veteran backup.</p>
<h4>
<a class="sbn-auto-link" href="http://www.sbnation.com/nfl-draft">NFL Draft</a> Options</h4>
<p>Copying and pasting what I wrote before: "There are a number of intriguing interior defensive line prospects in this draft, including A'Shawn Robinson, Robert Nkemdiche, and Andrew Billings. The Eagles have young and talent on the inside so they certainly don't need to force a pick. They could afford to draft some depth, though."</p>
