  <br>

    <h3>
        2/11/2016</h3>
    <div class="NewsBody">
        Many viewers of Sunday&#39;s Super Bowl 50 noticed the postgame &quot;free mentions&quot; Peyton Manning dropped about drinking Budweiser to celebrate the Denver Broncos&#39; win. Viewers in turn took to social media to talk about the brand mentions. &nbsp;<br />
<br />
But another brand was repeatedly shown throughout the game (without any official Super Bowl sponsorship), and achieved some prominent social media attention from it.&nbsp;<br />
<br />
Carolina Panthers coach Ron Rivera was shown throughout the telecast in his Transitions lenses. Transitions Optical, Inc., reported seeing an approximate 500% increase in social media mentions about his glasses.<br />
<br />
Among some of the tweets:<br />
<br />
@papermagazine (196,000 followers): I am all for Ron Rivera&#39;s transition lenses #goth<br />
<br />
@sammyrhodes (114,000 followers): I see you rocking those Transition lenses, Ron Rivera.<br />
<br />
@djacoby (78,000 followers): Shout to Ron Rivera&#39;s transitional lenses.<br />
<br />
@seanjentille (41,000 followers): Ron Rivera is trying to make history for people who wear transition lenses.&nbsp;<br />
<br />
@mapquest (25,000 followers): Ron Rivera is wearing transition lenses, Jonathan Stewart did the hand jive, clearly old trends are coming back in style (hint: we&#39;re next)<br />
<br />
&quot;For a brand that is not typically highly talked about during one of the biggest nights in sports, the social chatter helped up our own game and thrust us unexpectedly onto a national stage,&quot; says Patience Cook, director of North America Marketing for Transitions Optical. &quot;Much like the big game has evolved and celebrating 50 years of history, our team at Transitions has experienced decades of innovation that combines form and function&mdash;and obviously helpful to people like Ron when the versatility means the most.&quot;&nbsp;<br />
