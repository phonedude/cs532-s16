                                <div class="subscriber-preview"><p>Illinois House Democrats have revived an effort to send stalled contract talks to binding arbitration.</p></div><div class="subscriber-preview"><p>The move comes after Gov. Bruce Rauner’s administration has taken steps to have negotiations with the American Federation of State, County and Municipal Employees declared at an impasse.</p></div><div class="subscriber-only"><p>The bill, which would apply to contracts that expired June 30 and would sunset June 30, 2019, was approved by a House committee Thursday on a party-line vote.</p></div><div class="tnt-ads-container text-center hidden-sm hidden-md hidden-lg"><div class="tnt-ads dfp-ad" data-dfp-adunit="/8438/herald-review.com/news/state-and-regional/govt-and-politics" data-dfp-custom-pos="fixed-big-ad-top, atf" id="fixed-big-ad-top-mobile" data-dfp-size="[300,250]"></div></div>
    <div class="subscriber-only"><p>Rep. Chris Welch, a Hillside Democrat who’s sponsoring the bill, said it represents a major concession on the part of AFSCME, whose contract expired June 30, because the union would be giving up its right to strike.</p></div><div class="subscriber-only"><p>But Republican opponents of the bill said the union is attempting change the rules in the middle of the process.</p></div><div class="subscriber-only"><p>Before the committee hearing began Thursday morning, Gov. Bruce Rauner’s administration distributed a statement attacking the bill.</p></div><style>
