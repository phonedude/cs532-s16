                <h1 class="article-title">The best way to get to single-digit body fat</h1>

                
                    <p class="article-byline">
                        Are you finding a six-pack hard to come by? Bring it out with these tips
                    </p>
                

                <p class="article-meta has-image">
                    <a class="article-meta--avatar" href="/author/Men's-Health" title="Men's Health">
                        <img src="/assets/images/author-logo.jpg" alt="Men's Health" />

                    <time class="article-meta--published" pubdate datetime="10-10-February" title="10 Wed February 2016">Posted on February 10, 2016</time>
                </p>

                        <p class="image-credit"></p>

                

                <p>Obtaining the coveted single-digit percentage of body fat is a lonely path to walk, reserved for bodybuilders, superhero actors and the truly dedicated. If you&rsquo;re looking for jaw-dropping abs to go along with your gains, it&rsquo;s going to be tough. So how do bodybuilders drop themselves to that level?</p>

<p><a href="http://www.menshealth.co.uk/food-nutrition/muscle-foods/eat-like-a-bodybuilder"><strong>(Related: how to eat like a bodybuilder)</strong></a></p>

<h2>Carb smart</h2>

<p>The latest nutritional guidelines have shown that <strong><a href="http://www.menshealth.co.uk/lose-weight/what-is-more-effective-for-weightloss-low-fat-or-low-carb">fat is in and carbs are out</a></strong> for a while now, but many are stuck on the old staple that carbs are necessary before a gym sesh. Dead wrong if you&rsquo;re gunning for single digits.</p>

<p>&ldquo;People need to get it out of their heads that fat makes you fat,&rdquo; says pro PT Jason Patmore. &ldquo;Carbohydrates release insulin, and when insulin levels are raised the body is actually prevented from using fat as fuel. The goal is to reduce your body to that state.&rdquo;</p>

<p>Cutting body fat means training your body to use fat stores as fuel, something it won&rsquo;t be doing while you&rsquo;re burning through pre-workout pasta. Patmore recommends a high-protein breakfast without carbs before hitting to the gym, only using carbs after training to improve the protein synthesis process &ndash; this is the reason most protein shakes have a mix of both protein and carbohydrates.</p>

<p><a href="http://www.menshealth.co.uk/food-nutrition/the-8-most-common-protein-shake-mistakes"><strong>(Related: your most common protein shake mistakes) </strong></a></p>

<p>Your session is going to suck because of the low energy reserves before your body adapts, but if you&rsquo;re set on that lonely number it&rsquo;ll all be worth it to watch the fat melt away. Eating your carbs later in the day is more beneficial in a low-calorie diet, as the journal <em>Obesity (Silver Spring) </em>has shown that carbs late in the day reduce hunger pangs and improve weight loss.</p>

<p><img alt="" src="//assets.menshealth.co.uk/main/assets/Media_Pack/six_pack.jpg?mtime=1455120935" style="width: 100%; height: 100%;" /></p>

<h2>Myth buster</h2>

<p>One of the biggest body-fat myths around is that you can &ldquo;spot reduce&rdquo; fat by targeting certain body areas. For example, if you want six-pack abs, you&rsquo;ll be able to target subcutaneous fat (belly fat) specifically for faster results. But Patmore knows better.</p>

<p>&ldquo;Can you spot reduce?&rdquo; He asks incredulously. &ldquo;Your body will pile on or lose fat, but spot reducing is a myth. Certain areas of your body are more likely to store fat, so general fat loss will equate to losing flab in certain areas more than others.&rdquo; No targeting your double-chin, we&rsquo;re afraid: push through the pain and watch it melt away with the rest of your body.</p>

<h2>Keep going</h2>

<p>Operating at a calorie deficit, carefully managing your carbs and harvesting fat-stores during workouts will begin to drag your body fat down below the decade. All that remains is to roll up your sleeves and get on with the workouts &ndash; and for this, Patmore recommends upper and lower-body supersets followed by a steady-state run.</p>

<p><a href="http://www.menshealth.co.uk/building-muscle/6-best-dumbbell-exercises-for-hiit"><strong>(Related: the HIIT dumbbell workout)</strong></a></p>

<p>The following supersets, 15 reps of each with 2 mins rest between supersets, should kick your fat-loss journey into overdrive. We&rsquo;ll see you on the cover of the mag next year.</p>

<p><strong>Superset 1:<br />
<br />
<img alt="" src="//assets.menshealth.co.uk/main/assets/squatKB.gif?mtime=1442398904" style="width: 628px; height: 353px;" /></strong></p>

<ul>
	<li>Goblet squat: Stand with your legs slightly wider than shoulder-width apart, clasping a kettlebell in each hand in front of your chest with palms facing each other.</li>
	<li>Bend your knees and lower yourself into a squat, keeping the kettlebells in the same position and ensuring you don&#39;t round your black by tensing your glutes throughout. Drive back up and repeat.&nbsp;&nbsp;</li>
</ul>

<p><img alt="" src="//assets.menshealth.co.uk/main/assets/how-to-do-the-seated-shoulder-press-dumbbell.gif?mtime=1453220285" style="width: 628px; height: 419px;" /></p>

<ul>
	<li>Dumbbell shoulder press:&nbsp;Sit on the bench holding two dumbbells at shoulder height with an overhand grip.</li>
	<li>Press the weights up above your head until your arms are fully extended. Return slowly to the start position.</li>
</ul>

<p><br />
<strong>Superset 2:</strong></p>

<p><strong><img alt="" src="//assets.menshealth.co.uk/main/assets/bench-press-dumbell.gif?mtime=1447764573" style="width: 628px; height: 353px;" /></strong></p>

<ul>
	<li>Dumbbell bench press:&nbsp;Lie on a flat bench holding two dumbbells over your chest with an overhand grip.</li>
	<li>Push up until your arms are straight, then lower under control.</li>
</ul>

<p><img alt="" src="//assets.menshealth.co.uk/main/assets/splitty.gif?mtime=1432138990" style="width: 628px; height: 353px;" /></p>

<ul>
	<li>Dumbbell split squat: With a dumbbell in each hand, stand facing away from bench with one leg resting on it, laces down.</li>
	<li>Squat down with your standing leg until the knee of your trailing leg almost touches the floor. Push up through your front foot to return to the start position.</li>
</ul>

<p><br />
Follow that lot up with a steady state run at 65% intensity for 5km.</p>

<p><img alt="" src="//assets.menshealth.co.uk/main/assets/how-to-run-on-an-inclined-treadmill.gif?mtime=1453898031" style="width: 628px; height: 419px;" /></p>
