<h1 class="entry-title">MySpace Still Exists, Parent Company Sells Social Network&#8217;s Remains To Time&nbsp;Inc.</h1> 
<div class="post-info">By <span class="author vcard"><span class="fn"><a href="http://consumerist.com/author/marybethquirkconsumerist/" title="Mary Beth Quirk" rel="author">Mary Beth Quirk</a></span></span>   <span class="date published time" title="2016-02-11T13:16:55+00:00">February 11, 2016</span> </div><div class="entry-content"><p><a href="https://consumermediallc.files.wordpress.com/2016/02/screen-shot-2016-02-11-at-1-07-25-pm.png" rel="attachment wp-att-10231673" target="_blank"><img src="https://consumermediallc.files.wordpress.com/2016/02/screen-shot-2016-02-11-at-1-07-25-pm.png?w=680&#038;h=299" alt="Screen Shot 2016-02-11 at 1.07.25 PM" width="680" height="299" class="alignnone size-large wp-image-10231673" /></a>Prepare to transport yourself back to 2003, kids, before Facebook, when a profile meant a list of your Top 5 friends and a flashy, custom design, and MySpace ruled the social media realm (Friendster, sit down). Apparently, it still exists today, and Time Inc. now owns what&#8217;s left of it.<span id="more-10231665"></span></p>
<p>Parent company <a href="http://www.businesswire.com/news/home/20160211005412/en/Time-Acquire-Assets-Viant-Data-Driven-Leader-People-Based" target="_blank" target="_blank">Viant sold MySpace</a> &#8212; now dedicated to music and videos, though users still have profiles and a list of &#8220;top&#8221; friends  &#8212; to the magazine publisher behind <em>People</em>, <em>Sports Illustrated</em>, and <em>Time</em>, among others (h/t <a href="http://bigstory.ap.org/article/0b25a7ce0f054ede9158ecc3a241c6e0/magazine-publisher-time-inc-buys-whats-left-myspace" target="_blank" target="_blank">Associated Press</a>).</p>
<p>The price wasn&#8217;t disclosed, but Viant promises it can give marketers access to more than 1.2 billion users.</p>
<p>In 2008, the AP notes, MySpace boasted 76 million U.S. visitors before Facebook pushed it out of public favor. Justin Timberlake and some other investors <a href="http://consumerist.com/2011/06/30/news-corp-dumps-myspace-for-35-million/" target="_blank">bought it in 2011 from News Corp. for $35 million</a>.</p>
<p><a href="http://bigstory.ap.org/article/0b25a7ce0f054ede9158ecc3a241c6e0/magazine-publisher-time-inc-buys-whats-left-myspace" target="_blank" target="_blank">Magazine publisher Time Inc. buys what&#8217;s left of MySpace </a>[Associated Press]</p>
