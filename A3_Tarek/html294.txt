U.S. Senator Joni Ernst (R-IA), an original co-sponsor along with Senator Cory Booker (D-NJ), highlighted the critical importance of the legislation introduced by Senator Ron Johnson (R-WI).

&amp;ldquo;I want to thank both Chairman Johnson and Senator Booker for working with me on this bill. This will require the Obama Administration to fulfill a promise that they made back in 2011 to develop a comprehensive strategy to counter and prevent violent extremism and online radicalization,&amp;rdquo; Senator Ernst stated. &amp;ldquo;So while President Obama continues to lack a comprehensive overall strategy to defeat ISIS, this legislation will ensure he must at least present a strategy to address its use of social media to spread terror.&amp;rdquo;

Click here or on the image below to watch Senator Ernst&amp;rsquo;s remarks.



In December 2011, the Obama Administration released a &amp;ldquo;Strategic Implementation Plan for Empowering Local Partners to Prevent Violent Extremism in the United States&amp;rdquo; which made the following commitment: &amp;ldquo;[b]ecause of the importance of the digital environment, we will develop a separate, more comprehensive strategy for countering and preventing violent extremist online radicalization and leveraging technology to empower community resilience that considers: (1) the latest assessment of the role of the Internet; (2) the absence of clear national boundaries in online space and the relationship between international and domestic radicalization to violence; (3) relevant legal issues; and (4) the differing authorities and capabilities of departments and agencies.&amp;rdquo;

The Combat Terrorist Use of Social Media Act requires the president to prepare:


A report on terrorists&amp;rsquo; and terrorist organizations&amp;rsquo; use of social media and an evaluation of current government efforts to combat such use.
A comprehensive strategy to counter terrorists&amp;rsquo; and terrorist organizations&amp;rsquo; use of social media.

The bipartisan legislation is the companion to H.R. 3654, introduced by Rep. Ted Poe (R-TX) along with House Foreign Affairs Committee Chairman Ed Royce (R-CA) and Ranking Member Eliot Engel (D-NY), and passed by the House in December 2015.

<h1 class="title"><a href="/public/index.cfm/press-releases?ID=010F7316-86B3-4CCD-9E2F-4BFE63687C60">Ernst-Backed Bill to Combat Terrorist Use of Social Media Passes Committee</a></h1>
<h2 class="subtitle">Legislation awaits full Senate consideration</h2>
	
<h4 class="byline-date">






	
		
			<span class="date">
		
		
			
		
		<span class="month">Feb</span> <span class="day">10</span> <span class="year">2016</span></span>

<div class="content">
	
	
	
	
	
		
		<p><strong>WASHINGTON, D.C. &ndash;</strong> Today the <a href="http://www.hsgac.senate.gov/hearings/business-meeting-02/10/2016">Senate Homeland Security &amp; Governmental Affairs Committee</a> passed the <em>Combat Terrorist Use of Social Media Act of 2016 (S. 2517)</em>. U.S. Senator Joni Ernst (R-IA), an original co-sponsor along with Senator Cory Booker (D-NJ), highlighted the critical importance of the legislation introduced by Senator Ron Johnson (R-WI).</p>
<p><em>&ldquo;I want to thank both Chairman Johnson and Senator Booker for working with me on this bill. This will require the Obama Administration to fulfill a promise that they made back in 2011 to develop a comprehensive strategy to counter and prevent violent extremism and online radicalization,&rdquo;</em> <strong>Senator Ernst stated</strong>.<em> &ldquo;So while President Obama continues to lack a comprehensive overall strategy to defeat ISIS, this legislation will ensure he must at least present a strategy to address its use of social media to spread terr</em>or.&rdquo;</p>
<p style="text-align: center;"><em>Click <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0rsw74Gi1Cs&amp;feature=youtu.be">here</a> or on the image below to watch Senator Ernst&rsquo;s remarks.</em></p>
<p style="text-align: center;"><a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0rsw74Gi1Cs&amp;feature=youtu.be"><img src="http://www.ernst.senate.gov/public/index.cfm?a=Files.Serve&amp;File_id=35E7514D-C346-4CAF-9995-D488CC43D944" alt="HSGAC hearing terror social media" height="131" width="257" /></a></p>
<p>In December 2011, the Obama Administration released a <a href="https://www.whitehouse.gov/sites/default/files/sip-final.pdf">&ldquo;Strategic Implementation Plan for Empowering Local Partners to Prevent Violent Extremism in the United States&rdquo; </a>which made the following commitment: <em>&ldquo;[b]ecause of the importance of the digital environment, we will develop a separate, more comprehensive strategy for countering and preventing violent extremist online radicalization and leveraging technology to empower community resilience that considers: (1) the latest assessment of the role of the Internet; (2) the absence of clear national boundaries in online space and the relationship between international and domestic radicalization to violence; (3) relevant legal issues; and (4) the differing authorities and capabilities of departments and agencies.&rdquo;</em></p>
<p>The <em>Combat Terrorist Use of Social Media Act</em> requires the president to prepare:</p>
<ul>
<li>A report on terrorists&rsquo; and terrorist organizations&rsquo; use of social media and an evaluation of current government efforts to combat such use.</li>
<li>A comprehensive strategy to counter terrorists&rsquo; and terrorist organizations&rsquo; use of social media.</li>
</ul>
<p>The bipartisan legislation is the companion to H.R. 3654, introduced by Rep. Ted Poe (R-TX) along with House Foreign Affairs Committee Chairman Ed Royce (R-CA) and Ranking Member Eliot Engel (D-NY), and passed by the House in December 2015.</p>

<script>
jQuery(function($){
	// activate
	$('.office-regions').on('click', '[data-region-activate]', function(){
		var region = $(this).attr('data-region-activate');
		
		$('.office-regions').attr('data-region-active', region); // .data('active', region)
		
		$('.address-accordion dt a').removeClass('active');
		$('.address-accordion dd').removeClass('in');
		
		$('.address-accordion dt a.address-' + region + '-header').addClass('active');
		$('.address-accordion dd.address-' + region + '-content').addClass('in');
		
		return false;
	});
	
	// peek
	$('.office-regions').on('mouseenter', '[data-region-activate]', function(){
		var region = $(this).attr('data-region-activate') || null;
		
		if(region){
			$('.office-regions').attr('data-region-peek', region);
		}
		
		return false;
	}).on('mouseleave', '[data-region-activate]', function(){
		$('.office-regions').attr('data-region-peek', '');
		
		return false;
	});
});
</script>
