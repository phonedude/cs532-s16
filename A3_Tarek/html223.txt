			<div class="entry-content hyphenate" itemprop="articleBody">
				<p>Nearly 133 million Americans have some sort of chronic illness, and that number is expected to <a href="http://invisibleillnessweek.com/media-toolkit/statistics/" target="_blank">reach 157 million by 2020</a>, according to data on the Invisible Illness Week website.</p>
<p>From exhaustion to migraines to fatigue, many chronic illness symptoms aren&#8217;t visible to the naked eye, which makes them even more difficult to diagnose &#8212; and understand.</p>
<p>With a growing number of people affected by chronic illnesses like <a href="http://www.fmaware.org/about-fibromyalgia/symptoms/" target="_blank">fibromyalgia</a>, <a href="http://www.arthritis.org/about-arthritis/understanding-arthritis/what-is-arthritis.php" target="_blank">arthritis</a>, <a href="https://www.lymedisease.org/lyme-basics/lyme-disease/about-lyme/" target="_blank">Lyme disease</a>, <a href="http://www.ccfa.org/what-are-crohns-and-colitis/what-is-crohns-disease/?referrer=https://www.google.com/" target="_blank">Crohn’s disease</a> and <a href="http://www.diabetes.org/diabetes-basics/" target="_blank">diabetes</a> and more, it&#8217;s important to both raise awareness and let others know how to respond to the needs of individuals living with these conditions.</p>
<p>So we asked readers <a href="https://www.facebook.com/Themightysite/posts/578356588978759" target="_blank">in our Facebook community</a> what <a href="https://www.facebook.com/ChronicIllnessOnThemighty/posts/1062143227140758" target="_blank">truths about chronic illness</a> they wish others understood. Here&#8217;s what they had to say:</p>
<p><strong>1.</strong> “Some days you can function, some you can’t.” —<a href="https://www.facebook.com/ChronicIllnessOnThemighty/posts/1062143227140758?comment_id=1062171717137909&amp;comment_tracking=%7B%22tn%22%3A%22R9%22%7D" target="_blank">Amber Wandmaker</a></p>
<p><img class="aligncenter" src="http://themighty.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/02/new-function-750x499.jpg" alt="Woman on beach" /></p>
<p><strong>2.</strong> “I&#8217;m not lazy. I&#8217;m in pain, exhausted and quite possibly depressed because I feel useless and cannot make others understand what I go through on a daily basis.” —<a href="https://www.facebook.com/ChronicIllnessOnThemighty/posts/1062143227140758?comment_id=1062565653765182&amp;comment_tracking=%7B%22tn%22%3A%22R%22%7D" target="_blank">Jodie Farber Brubacher</a></p>
<p><strong>3.</strong> “It&#8217;s not in my head.” —<a href="https://www.facebook.com/ChronicIllnessOnThemighty/posts/1062143227140758?comment_id=1062343483787399&amp;comment_tracking=%7B%22tn%22%3A%22R4%22%7D" target="_blank">Christine Olson Smith</a></p>
<p><img class="aligncenter" src="http://themighty.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/02/new-inmyhead-750x497.jpg" alt="Writing on notepad" /></p>
<p><strong>4.</strong> “When I&#8217;m pushing through&#8230; I&#8217;m really pushing myself too hard.” —<a href="https://www.facebook.com/ChronicIllnessOnThemighty/posts/1062143227140758?comment_id=1062346727120408&amp;comment_tracking=%7B%22tn%22%3A%22R3%22%7D" target="_blank">Debra Declue</a></p>
<p><strong>5.</strong> “Just because you can&#8217;t see it doesn&#8217;t mean it&#8217;s not real.” —<a href="https://www.facebook.com/ChronicIllnessOnThemighty/posts/1062143227140758?comment_id=1062237537131327&amp;comment_tracking=%7B%22tn%22%3A%22R6%22%7D" target="_blank">Rhonda Miller-Solomon</a></p>
<p><img class="aligncenter" src="http://themighty.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/02/new-seeit-750x501.jpg" alt="Magnifying glass, close-up, cropped" /></p>
<p><strong>6.</strong> “Accepting the fact that I will never get better is what has allowed me to live my life and continue to work towards my goals without waiting to &#8216;get better.’” —<a href="https://www.facebook.com/ChronicIllnessOnThemighty/posts/1062143227140758?comment_id=1062220233799724&amp;comment_tracking=%7B%22tn%22%3A%22R8%22%7D" target="_blank">Joan Elizabeth</a></p>
<p><strong>7.</strong> “A good day for people with chronic pain&#8230; is often just ‘less of a bad day.’” —<a href="https://www.facebook.com/ChronicIllnessOnThemighty/posts/1062143227140758?comment_id=1062202120468202&amp;comment_tracking=%7B%22tn%22%3A%22R9%22%7D" target="_blank">Ann Webb Bradford</a></p>
<p><strong>8.</strong> “Being immune compromised/suppressed isn&#8217;t a game; it&#8217;s dangerous. When I say I can&#8217;t get sick, I&#8217;m not being paranoid; I&#8217;m being careful. Life&#8217;s no good when you&#8217;re dead!” —<a href="https://www.facebook.com/ChronicIllnessOnThemighty/posts/1062143227140758?comment_id=1062229273798820&amp;comment_tracking=%7B%22tn%22%3A%22R7%22%7D" target="_blank">Arianna Nyswonger</a></p>
<p><strong>9.</strong> “I&#8217;ve become an expert on hiding my pain from everyone.” —<a href="https://www.facebook.com/ChronicIllnessOnThemighty/posts/1062143227140758?comment_id=1062193990469015&amp;comment_tracking=%7B%22tn%22%3A%22R9%22%7D" target="_blank">Beth Cox Harrell</a></p>
<p><img class="aligncenter" src="http://themighty.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/02/new-expert-750x500.jpg" alt="Woman looking through blinds" /></p>
<p><strong>10.</strong> “Chronic illness often comes in multiple versions; treating one can mean making another one worse.” —<a href="https://www.facebook.com/ChronicIllnessOnThemighty/posts/1062143227140758?comment_id=1062193653802382&amp;comment_tracking=%7B%22tn%22%3A%22R9%22%7D" target="_blank">Zoann Murphy</a></p>
<p><strong>11.</strong> “I can&#8217;t remember what ‘well’ feels like.” —<a href="https://www.facebook.com/ChronicIllnessOnThemighty/posts/1062143227140758?comment_id=1062190573802690&amp;comment_tracking=%7B%22tn%22%3A%22R9%22%7D" target="_blank">Sandra Williams</a></p>
<p><strong>12.</strong> “Good can change to bad in a matter of minutes. There are good days and bad days, but there are also good hours and bad hours, good minutes and bad minutes. Chronic illness does not see or understand time.” —<a href="https://www.facebook.com/ChronicIllnessOnThemighty/posts/1062143227140758?comment_id=1062181347136946&amp;comment_tracking=%7B%22tn%22%3A%22R9%22%7D" target="_blank">Deanna Guarino-Embry</a></p>
<p><img class="aligncenter" src="http://themighty.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/02/see-time-750x500.jpg" alt="Clock on wall" /></p>
<p><strong>13.</strong> “There&#8217;s no such thing as &#8216;too young;&#8217; we can&#8217;t just show out bodies ID and tell the illness to come back in 10-20 years!” —<a href="https://www.facebook.com/ChronicIllnessOnThemighty/posts/1062143227140758?comment_id=1062178473803900&amp;comment_tracking=%7B%22tn%22%3A%22R9%22%7D" target="_blank">Jen Andrew</a></p>
<p><strong>14.</strong> “It doesn&#8217;t get better. It is this way. Every day. Forever. If you&#8217;re in my life, please don&#8217;t ask me if I&#8217;m feeling &#8216;better&#8217; today<strong>.</strong>” —<a href="https://www.facebook.com/Themightysite/posts/578356588978759?comment_id=578625662285185&amp;comment_tracking=%7B%22tn%22%3A%22R9%22%7D" target="_blank">Wendy Rose</a></p>
<p><img class="aligncenter" src="http://themighty.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/02/feeling-better-750x498.jpg" alt="Old man feeling sick" /></p>
<p><strong>15.</strong> “We shouldn&#8217;t be treated like drug addicts or hypochondriacs and denied the care we truly need.” —<a href="https://www.facebook.com/Themightysite/posts/578356588978759?comment_id=578573312290420&amp;comment_tracking=%7B%22tn%22%3A%22R9%22%7D" target="_blank">Amy Brandborg</a></p>
<p><strong>16.</strong> “I live a double life. What you see when I am at work: someone who is well put together, always dependable, smiling, will always tell you she is doing fine. My reality: I&#8217;m a mess, in so much pain, my smiles are forced, sometimes I have to find a quiet place to cry. But it&#8217;s much easier for me to fake it because coworkers don&#8217;t want to hear my truth day after day.” —<a href="https://www.facebook.com/Themightysite/posts/578356588978759?comment_id=578552425625842&amp;comment_tracking=%7B%22tn%22%3A%22R9%22%7D" target="_blank">Amber Weller</a></p>
<p><img class="aligncenter" src="http://themighty.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/02/double-life-750x500.jpg" alt="Woman in front of mirror" /></p>
<p><strong>17.</strong> “A ‘good attitude’ doesn&#8217;t take away pain or improve mobility. I am still human and I will have days when I just can&#8217;t drum up that ‘good attitude.’” —<a href="https://www.facebook.com/Themightysite/posts/578356588978759?comment_id=578555045625580&amp;comment_tracking=%7B%22tn%22%3A%22R9%22%7D" target="_blank">Vicki Gomes Petilli</a></p>
<p><img class="aligncenter" src="http://themighty.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/02/new-good-750x563.jpg" alt="Woman standing in field barefoot" /></p>
<p><strong>18.</strong> “I didn&#8217;t do this to myself. I didn&#8217;t not pray hard enough or believe hard enough. I didn&#8217;t not eat well enough or not exercise enough.” —<a href="https://www.facebook.com/Themightysite/posts/578356588978759?comment_id=578776868936731&amp;comment_tracking=%7B%22tn%22%3A%22R9%22%7D" target="_blank">Barb Silvestro</a></p>
<p><strong>19.</strong> “It hurts to be forgotten because we have said no to so many events, parties, family gatherings, shopping trips, etc. I want them to still ask just so I know they are still thinking of me.” —<a href="https://www.facebook.com/Themightysite/posts/578356588978759?comment_id=578604072287344&amp;comment_tracking=%7B%22tn%22%3A%22R9%22%7D" target="_blank">Victoria Sinclair</a></p>
<p><img class="aligncenter" src="http://themighty.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/02/forgotten-750x488.jpg" alt="Upset woman with grey hair" /></p>
<p><strong>20.</strong> “There is no magic cure. I don&#8217;t want to be sick, but this is my reality. I can&#8217;t wish it away or cleanse it away or take supplements because your sister&#8217;s boyfriend&#8217;s mother did.” —<a href="https://www.facebook.com/Themightysite/posts/578356588978759?comment_id=578552345625850&amp;comment_tracking=%7B%22tn%22%3A%22R9%22%7D" target="_blank">Christina Marroquin-Mauricio</a></p>
<p><img class="aligncenter" src="http://themighty.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/02/magic-cure-750x563.jpg" alt="Spoon with heart in it" /></p>
<p><strong>21.</strong> “People don&#8217;t choose to feel horrible every day and lose the person they used to be.” &#8211;<a href="https://www.facebook.com/Themightysite/posts/578356588978759?comment_id=578541212293630&amp;comment_tracking=%7B%22tn%22%3A%22R9%22%7D" target="_blank">Caitlin Hoechst</a></p>
<p><strong>22.</strong> “What I can do one day I might not be able to do the next.” —<a href="https://www.facebook.com/Themightysite/posts/578356588978759?comment_id=578628215618263&amp;comment_tracking=%7B%22tn%22%3A%22R9%22%7D" target="_blank">Becky Rider</a></p>
<p><strong>23.</strong> “I’m not making it up. If I wanted to fake an illness, I would choose something that people would believe!” —<a href="https://www.facebook.com/Themightysite/posts/578356588978759?comment_id=578559685625116&amp;comment_tracking=%7B%22tn%22%3A%22R9%22%7D" target="_blank">Faith Merryn</a></p>
<p><img class="aligncenter" src="http://themighty.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/02/new-fake-750x500.jpg" alt="Upset man" /></p>
<p><strong>24</strong>. “My downtime doesn&#8217;t mean I&#8217;m depressed or isolating. It&#8217;s a time for me to heal and recharge.” —<a href="https://www.facebook.com/Themightysite/posts/578356588978759?comment_id=578731845607900&amp;comment_tracking=%7B%22tn%22%3A%22R3%22%7D" target="_blank">Kate Wilhelmi</a></p>
<p><strong>25</strong>. “My illness does not define me, my dreams or who I am!” —<a href="https://www.facebook.com/ChronicIllnessOnThemighty/posts/1062143227140758?comment_id=1062201720468242&amp;comment_tracking=%7B%22tn%22%3A%22R9%22%7D" target="_blank">Judy Fox Berryan</a></p>
