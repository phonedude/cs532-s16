        <p>Despite her age, Jamie Silvonek was charged as an adult for the crime. She entered her plea Thursday in front of Judge Maria Dantos.</p><p>Police said they found forensic evidence on Jamie Silvonek's cell phone that shows she talked with Barnes, then 21, about killing her mother.</p><p>"I want her gone," said one message from Jamie Silvonek to Barnes.</p><p>Officials said, through their investigation, witnesses told police Jamie Silvonek wanted to kill both of her parents.</p><p>Police said Jamie Silvonek lied about her age to Barnes and asked him to lie about his age to her mother.  </p><p>When her mother found out Barnes' true age, she ordered Barnes and Jamie Silvonek to end their relationship.</p><p>Immediately, Jamie Silvonek's conversations with Barnes included discussions of her plans to have Barnes kill her parents and then have the two of them dispose of their bodies, police said.</p><p>One week later, Barnes, who was stationed at Fort Meade, Maryland, traveled to the Silvoneks home. The pair had tickets to a concert in Scranton that evening.</p><p>Conversations via phone calls and text that day included Jamie Silvonek's mother adamantly telling the teen she was not allowed to have Barnes at the house.</p><p>Jamie Silvonek, police said, texted Barnes, "She needs to go, Caleb. Right now. You don't understand." And later that day, she texted, "She threatened to throw me out of the house.  I want her gone.  I just need to you [sic] be able to come over so we can do whatever necessary, honestly."</p><p>When Barnes arrived at the house, Cheryl Silvonek tried to prove her daughter's age to Barnes using her passport.  </p><p>Cheryl Silvonek then texted her husband, who didn't know about his daughter's relationship, that she would be driving Jamie Silvonek and her "friend" to Scranton for a concert.</p><p>Later in the afternoon, Jamie Silvonek texted Barnes that he should kill Cheryl while Jamie was in the bathroom.  She suggested waiting until they were in the car because that would be "easier" for him, police investigations show.</p><p>He replied, "fine."</p><p>The two continued their discussion via text message as Cheryl drove north on I-476. They then attempted to clean Cheryl's car and drove to South Whitehall Township to dispose of her body.</p><p>They buried her in a shallow grave, police said.</p><p>Barking dogs alerted a neighbor to Cheryl Silvonek's unoccupied car along Haasadahl Road. The person saw blood inside the car and called 911.</p></div>
