Manning, Newton, the Super Bowl, and the legality of online betting

Brady McAninch
February 7, 2016

Today is treated as a national holiday in the United States.  Everywhere people will gather with family and friends to chow down on wings, laugh at commercials, and watch the Super Bowl.  Super Bowl Sunday is also one of the most active gambling days of the year.  This year, it is estimated that over 4 billion dollars in bets will be made about the big game.  People can bet on anything from who will be the MVP of the game to whether Peyton Manning the “Sheriff” will retire immediately following the game. 

The question becomes, is gambling on the game legal?  The answer to that question is much more difficult than one may think.  Throughout the majority of the U.S. gambling is outlawed, including in Illinois and Missouri (other than a few exceptions such as casinos, the lottery, and certain gaming machines).  In fact, gambling is outlawed in nearly every state and frowned upon by various federal laws and regulations.  However, the online nature of betting and gambling muddies the waters as the internet is non-physical and, thus, the sports betting websites can be technically “located” or “headquartered” outside of the United States.  This creates massive problems for enforcement of the anti-gambling laws as most of them are aimed at the institutions not the individuals.  Therefore, if the gambling websites are located outside of the United States, they can avoid the purview of these laws and their enforcement.  

This means that although the betting or gambling being done may still be illegal, or at least frowned upon, that the enforcement of these laws and regulations is difficult, if not impossible.  Thus, anyone interested in betting online should be aware that these laws exist and, despite the lack of enforcement of these laws to date, that does not mean that the actual betting is legal.  This is even true of many NCAA office pools and other acts the majority of Americans consider common place. 

Regardless, everyone at Hipskind & McAninch, LLC, hopes that you enjoy this unofficial holiday with your family and friends.
