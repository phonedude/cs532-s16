Would a Broken Arm Stop You?
288
By Dave Meltzer

To say that the story of Thomas Davis is a great tale of perseverance does not quite do it justice. The man who is planning to play this weekend’s Super Bowl with a broken arm has fought adversity his entire sporting career, and shown the focus and will to push past it. Much like a blade of grass breaking the surface of the Earth, Thomas Davis pushed through resistance he has faced. He has come out the other side as a victor, and is now just one win away from being a Super Bowl Champion.

Davis faced long odds from the beginning of his football career, playing at Randolph-Clay High School in rural Georgia. Though he was a very successful four-sport athlete, and played as many as eight different positions for his high school football team, Thomas Davis had no offers to play Division 1 college football. It was not until UGA defensive Coach Brian VanGorder saw Thomas Davis at a basketball practice that he got his one and only Division 1 offer. He had the motivation and the drive to #keepcompeting and kept at it. He had a dream and the dedication and determination to get there.

He went on to resounding success at UGA as a linebacker and safety, earning All-SEC honors and being drafted in the first round by the Carolina Panthers. During his fifth year in the NFL, however, Thomas Davis’ medical issues began. First it was discovered that he has an abnormality that causes him to have both two coronary arteries on the same side of his heart. Luckily, he was able to avoid having surgery due to his excellent health. Unfortunately, not long after learning this is when Thomas Davis suffered his first torn ACL. The surgeon used some of his hamstring tendon to strengthen the ligament, but it tore again during that same offseason. Not to be deterred, Davis went in for another surgery, this time taking tendon from his left knee to try and prevent future injury. In 2011, for the third time in three years, Thomas Davis tore the anterior cruciate ligament in his right knee.
 
He could have easily called it quits and retired. There had never been an NFL player who had come back from 3 ACL tears in the same knee. Instead, Davis chose to revive and thrive to continue competing. He vowed to come back strong from his injury, and inspire hope among others. And come back strong he did! This year was his fourth straight season eclipsing the 100 tackle mark, and he even earned his first Pro Bowl and All-Pro honors. He was also acknowledged for his community service work, winning the Walter Payton Man of the Year Award in 2014.

Though he is having a wonderful year, in typical Thomas Davis fashion, this season is not without its obstacles. Davis broke his arm in the Panthers’ NFC Championship victory, and had surgery almost immediately. Instead of creating more shortages, voids and obstacles and making himself a victim, he stood resolute and was able to tap into his motivation to #keepcompeting. Motivation is a key component to high-level competition. And as always, Thomas Davis changes the way he looks at things and the things he looks at change. So, of course, a broken arm isn’t going to stop him. Davis is adamant he will play in the Super Bowl, proving once again that the only person who manifests obstacles, voids and shortages is the only person who can overcome or release them as well! That person is You!!
