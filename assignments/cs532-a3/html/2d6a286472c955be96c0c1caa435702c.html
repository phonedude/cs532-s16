Murray’s record-setting day leads UK to blowout win over Florida

    • By Metz Camfield on February 6, 2016
  • Tweet

When it was announced just minutes prior to tipoff Saturday that Alex Poythress would not be able to play due to an injury to his right, non-surgically repaired knee, anxiety swept over the Big Blue Nation.

Kentucky, attempting to avoid its first three-game losing streak ever under head coach John Calipari, was coming off a loss at Tennessee in which it blew a 21-point, first-half lead, its largest in the Calipari era. What ensued was a much-improved performance by a Kentucky team needing it.

“It was a great bounce-back game,” Calipari said.

The Wildcats (17-6, 7-3 Southeastern Conference) scored on seven of their first nine possessions of the game and led by double digits for all but three minutes and 24 seconds en route to winning 80-61 and moving to within a game of first place in the SEC.

“They were so good against Tennessee early, and I could see that we just saw that for 40 minutes,” Florida head coach Mike White said. “… If you let a guy like Jamal Murray really get going early and he’s got his mojo going, and (Tyler) Ulis is playing like he did today, they’re capable of beating anyone in college basketball.”

Murray was on fire from beginning to end Saturday, tying a UK freshman scoring record with 35 points, including eight 3-pointers on just 10 attempts. The Kitchener, Ontario, native’s eight made 3-point field goals tied a record for a player, regardless of class, in the Calipari era.

Jamal Murray tied a UK freshman scoring record with 35 points Saturday, becoming the first UK freshman ever to have multiple 30-point games. (photo by Chris Reynolds)

“I knew he had a lot of points,” Coach Cal said. “He knew he had a lot.”

Murray hit his first six 3-pointers of the game, including all five in the first half, but contends that he wasn’t paying attention to how many points he had until the end. The rookie’s 35 points makes him the first freshman with multiple 30-point performances in school history.

“Jamal Murray stole the show with his ability to make shots,” White said. “I think he’s still open right now. I’ve never been a part of anything like that; very, very discouraged. It’s like we didn’t know that he’s a good shooter. Not a very good effort. I wish we would have given Jamal a better effort. He was unbelievable. He’s a really good shooter and they did a great job of getting him open.”

Not to be outdone, Ulis logged his third double-double of the year with 18 points and a career-high 11 assists, joining former UK star John Wall as the only players under Calipari with 10-plus assists in multiple games. Together, Ulis and Murray combined to score 30 of UK’s first 39 points.

Prior to the game, Calipari told his sophomore point guard that he’d have an opportunity for 15 assists against the Gators with the way they play defensively. While he came up just shy of that gaudy mark, he impressed yet another opposing coach and strengthened his season averages to 6.4 assists per game with a 3.2 assist-to-turnover ratio. Ulis is now the first Wildcat with at least four assists in 16 consecutive games since Roger Harden in 1985-86.

“I thought Ulis really controlled the entire first half, offensively and defensively,” White said. “He didn’t allow our guards to even get into ball screens defensively. I thought he put on a clinic, some of those things that won’t show up in the box score offensively. He runs the show.”

Tyler Ulis logged his third double-double of the year with 18 points and a career-high 11 assists against Florida. (photo by Chris Reynolds)

In the frontcourt, freshman forward Isaac Humphries stepped up in Poythress’ absence. After playing just 16 minutes in the previous 12 games, including not playing in eight games, Humphries logged 15 minutes against the Gators, scored four points, grabbed six rebounds, including three offensive, and blocked two shots.

“He earned his space, and I’m so happy for him because he has worked so hard and hasn’t had the opportunity, and what he did was he took advantage, and we all look and say, he needs to play,” Coach Cal said. “Let him play. And then you’ve got to stop waiting on some other guys, you’ve got to say, hey, he deserves it.”

Humphries found out he’d get the start just minutes prior to the opening tip, and said he was “on a mission” to prove himself Saturday.

“I finally got an opportunity and now I’ve got to continue growing on that,” Humphries said. “In Coach’s eyes he will see my progression and he’ll be more comfortable with playing me.”

In practice Thursday and Friday, the Wildcats went after each other, including a two-and-a-half hour session Thursday in which they focused primarily on defense and rebounding. The end result was a game in which UK’s physicality “surprised” Florida, White said, and pleased Calipari.

Jamal Murray’s eight 3-pointers tie Eric Bledsoe for the most by a Wildcat under Coach Cal. (photo by Barry Westerman, UK Athletics)

After allowing 1.11 points per possession at Kansas, and a season-high 1.25 at Tennessee, Kentucky allowed just 0.94 points per possession to Florida. The Gators’ 61 total points marked their second lowest outing since Dec. 12, and was far below their 75.0 points per game average.

“That’s all we’ve been focusing on,” Ulis said of the Cats’ defense. “The last couple of games, we broke down a lot defensively and we’re trying to get back to where we were earlier. We understand as a team we can’t just win on offense.”

The win marked Kentucky’s 33rd in a row at Rupp Arena, tying the second longest winning streak in the arena’s history, and gave Kentucky plenty of confidence with eight games remaining in league action.

“To do this against this team – and again, you have to understand, this team beat West Virginia by 20, and they had won five of six games,” Coach Cal said. “So they had been playing well. But this was good for us, good win.”
