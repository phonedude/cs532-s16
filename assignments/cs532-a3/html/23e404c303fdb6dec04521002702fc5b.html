  • The buildup to Super Bowl 50 has allowed football fans to take a good look into the NFL’s past with repeated highlights from previous Super Bowls.
    I’ve decided to follow suit with my seventh annual Celebrity Super Bowl Prediction column by doing some reminiscing of my own and have decided to take a stroll down Memory Lane – 80s style.
    In honor of Super Bowl 50, I’ve collected 50 celebrity predictions (technically 48) on Sunday’s game, but this year I put an emphasis on the stars of the 80s and have had so much fun interacting with those I grew up with – Tiffany, Toni Basil, the original MTV VJs, Fred Lynn and a whole lot more.
    Tiffany even mentioned that football played a role in her becoming a singer.
    “My dad was a huge Rams fan,” she said. “He would have people over for football games. Our neighbors were country music singers and they would come over and sing. I was always singing in my bedroom, but never really sang in front of anyone. When we had these parties, I tried it and it helped me get going.”
    These days Tiffany is still making music and is ready to embark on an 80s cruise at the end of the month where she will be joined by Huey Lewis & the News, Richard Marx, Flock of Seagulls and more.
    “I have a new record coming out the 31st of March,” she said. “I’ll be heading to England in June. I’m excited about the 80s cruise. I’ve never been on a cruise before, but I’m looking forward to it.”
    She also reflected on her days in the 80s.
    “The 80s were fun,” she said. “It was a little goofy and we thought we were cool, but it was a time where you could be unique or a plain Jane like me.”
    As for her prediction for Super Bowl 50 goes, Tiffany’s taking the Denver Broncos.
    “I have two brothers-in-law who are big Broncos fans,” she said. “Broncos 28, Panthers 14.”
    Sheila E, whose big hit ‘The Glamorous Life’ was a huge success in 1984, flaunted her football knowledge and gave an in-depth analysis of Super Bowl 50.
    “Carolina 24, Denver 20. People have been sleeping on Carolina all year,” she wrote. “Despite cruising to a 17-1 record, the majority of folks consistently bet against them week by week – as if they were lucky to keep winning. You can’t get lucky in the NFL and go 17-1.
    “I’m calling the comparison of defenses a wash. Both teams boast the best defenses in the league and I can’t argue that either way. They are both physical and fun to watch.
    Page 2 of 7 - “The difference is on the offensive side of the ball. Though part of me wants to see Peyton Manning finish his amazing career with his second Super Bowl, it seems obvious he is limping to the finish line. On the Carolina side, Cam Newton is playing the best ball of his career and is the likely MVP of the league. His throws are laser like and accurate. On the other hand, Peyton’s ball gets to where it needs to go, but slowly flutters there in the process. Carolina’s defensive speed can equate to a pick 6 real fast if Peyton’s deteriorating arm throws a few ducks out there. All throwing aside, it’s Cam’s legs that are the major difference. His ability to run and make something out of nothing is the main reason I’m giving the edge to Carolina. Cam doesn’t have the big game experience of Manning, but he doesn’t seem to get nervous out there. He plays loose and has fun doing it. Watch out world, it’s Cam time.”
    All I can say is, wow.
    Below are the remaining predictions from this year’s batch of celebrities. Not everyone is from the 80s, but we’ll start off with a heavy dose from the good ol’ days and end with a bold prediction from Christopher Knight of The Brady Bunch. Enjoy.
    CYNDI LAUPER: (singer): “The Broncos are going to win, I think they are the better team. Also, Peyton Manning deserves the win. He’s put in his time. He’s not just a great athlete, he’s a class act too. Peyton has led a exemplary life on and off the field and he is to be celebrated, admired and applauded. Go Broncos!” NO PREDICTED SCORE GIVEN.
    STEFAN DOROSCHUK (Men Without Hats): “Denver’s going to win mainly because their D stopped Brady, so stopping Newton shouldn’t be harder.” BRONCOS 28, PANTHERS 16.
    TONI BASIL (singer, choreographer, dancer): “I’m always as interested in the game as well as the halftime. I love to watch the ballet that’s going on the football field. I continue to be amazed how the quarterback and the wide receiver connect – it’s always thrilling to watch. I usually don’t root for one team or another, just for the guy who throwing the ball and the guy who’s catching it, but if you want a prediction I say it’s the Panthers.” PANTHERS 35, BRONCOS 14.
    JOHN OATES (singer, one half of Hall and Oates): “Having lived in Colorado for over 25 years my heart is with the Broncos but I know it will be a battle in the trenches and hope its a great game!” NO PREDICTED SCORE GIVEN.
    Page 3 of 7 - TOMMY HEATH (singer, Tommy Tutone): “If the Denver D can do the number on Brady and Belichek, then the Panthers are in for a long day.” BRONCOS 27, PANTHERS 17.
    WAYNE NELSON (Little River Band): “As the sole American added to Little River Band back in the day, I love football. I’m picking Denver by 4. I think Ware and company will get to Cam and rattle him. ( I’m a Cowboys fan and happen to love Demarcus Ware.)” NO PREDICTED SCORE GIVEN.
    RYAN RICKS (Little River Band): PANTHERS 30, BRONCOS 17.
    CHERYL TIEGS (supermodel): “Carolina consistently strong with offense AND defense. Also, Newton has a cuter a**.” PANTHERS 24, BRONCOS 21.
    FEE WAYBILL (singer, The Tubes): “Too many weapons for Carolina. Running, passing and CAM!!! The Broncos have a good defense but Peyton is done.” PANTHERS 27, BRONCOS 10.
    NINA BLACKWOOD (Original MTV VJ): “The Broncos will win due to the leadership of Peyton Manning and his collegian good looks, their strong defense and pass rush. Also, the Broncos sacked a lot of quarterbacks this last year. Go Broncos” BRONCOS 27, PANTHERS 21.
    ALAN HUNTER (Original MTV VJ): “Love Manning, but Cam Newton is just too damn good. Also, he hails from Auburn. War Eagle! (and Roll Tide! for my other Alabama family and friends).” PANTHERS 31, BRONCOS 24
    JACK NICKLAUS (Hall of Fame golfer): “My head tells me Carolina, but my heart says Denver. Peyton Manning is a friend and I am a big fan of him as a player and a person. No one knows if this will be his last game, but if it is, he deserves to go out on top. BRONCOS 28, PANTHERS 24.”
    FRANKIE BANALI (Quiet Riot drummer): “Carolina arguably has one of the best defenses in the league and then there’s Newton. The Panthers will eat Broncos – survival of the fittest. And I’m a vegetarian.” PANTHERS 27, BRONCOS 14.
    KIM CARNES (singer): “I am a huge football fan. I say Peyton and the Broncos win. Both teams have a strong defense but I have big faith in Denver’s defense. It would be especially great for Peyton to win this one.” BRONCOS 28, PANTHERS 24.
    FRED LYNN (Former Red Sox all-star outfielder): “I don’t think that Denver will be able to keep up with the Carolina offense. Both have good Ds but Carolinas running attack will keep those DBs honest and then the passing game will open up.” PANTHERS 31 BRONCOS 17.
    DAN JANSEN (Olympic speed skating gold medalist): “I have tons of respect for Peyton and I think Denver has a great defense. But the Panthers have great defense and a great offense. Newton will make too many plays for Denver to stop. And I just don’t see Denver moving the ball very well against the Panthers D. This one won’t be close.” PANTHERS 31. BRONCOS 14.
    Page 4 of 7 - FLORENCE HENDERSON (America’s favorite TV mom): “Cam Newton is a fierce competitor but I have to go with old pro – Peyton Manning. I think it will be close, however.” NO PREDICTED SCORE GIVEN.
    DAWN WELLS: (actress, Gilligan's Island): "I think the Carolina Panthers are probably going to win, but I'm picking Denver. This is probably going to be Peyton's last year because of his injuries so I think the Broncos are going to be more pressed to go out in style." BRONCOS 21, PANTHERS 17.
    LEE DEWYZE (American Idol Season 9 winner): “Peyton magic.” BRONCOS 24, PANTHERS 20.
    JOHN LEHR (actor, comedian www.howler.tv): “I know I am biased because Manning has humiliated my Chiefs for decades but I fear we are returning to the Super Bowl scores of the 90s. I have no doubt that Cam is actually a robot from the future and will pound the measly Bronco primates.” PANTHERS 35, BRONCOS 7.
    YURI SARDAROV (actor, Chicago Fire): “Everyone’s taking the Panthers, but I think Manning is going to will himself to go out on top. It’ll make a better movie.” BRONCOS 24, PANTHERS 20.
    NIK WALLENDA (high-wire artist): “Although I would love to see Peyton win one more ring. The Panthers have more “swagger” and an incredible offense that I believe will outshine the Broncos’ impressive defense. Carolina has proven that they are (almost) unbeatable this season. May the best team win.” PANTHERS 31, BRONCOS 14.
    MARK CUBAN (Dallas Mavericks owner): “Cam needs to make a few more commercials and the only way to get them is to win. He won’t be denied.” PANTHERS 24, BRONCOS 17.
    MARIO ANDRETTI (Hall of Fame race car driver and still the lone person to ever predict the correct score in seven years of this column): “I am predicting with my heart on this one. I want Peyton to go out on a high note.” BRONCOS 17, PANTHERS 13.
    SONIA MANZANO (actress, played Maria from Sesame Street and freely admits she doesn’t know much about football): “I definitely think the Carolina Panthers will win over the Denver Broncos because cats are much smarter than horses. You show a cat where the kitty litter is just once, and it knows where to “go.” Horses, not so much. Have you been around the horse carriages in Central Park? Disaster! As far as the what the score might be? Beats me! Cats never give themselves away until the last minute.” NO PREDICTED SCORE GIVEN.
    TONY VLACHOS (Survivor 2014 winner): “Although Denver’s D can disrupt Newton, they haven’t faced such a dynamic QB who can run and make plays on the fly like he does. Manning is a shell of his former self and doesn’t stand a chance against the almighty Newton.” PANTHERS 37, BRONCOS 21.
    Page 5 of 7 - ADAM CAROLLA (comedian): “Two words, one auto part: Cam Newton.” PANTHERS 28, BRONCOS 10
    BUTCH PATRICK (actor, played Eddie Munster): PANTHERS 38, BRONCOS 30.
    RICHARD KLINE (actor, Three’s Company): “The Carolina Panthers win SuperBowl 50. Mr. Newton has too many options at his disposal and the Carolina defense is highly underrated. Hopefully the first offensive snap for the Broncos will NOT go over Peyton’s head.” PANTHERS 38, BRONCOS 10.
    JACE NORMAN (actor, Nickelodeon’s Henry Danger): “I predict the Carolina Panthers are going to win Super Bowl 50. The Broncos defense is pretty stout but they can’t contain the animal that is Cam Newton.” PANTHERS 20, BRONCOS 16.
    ERIC ROBERTS (actor): “I want Peyton Manning to win his second Super Bowl ring, but I’m afraid the Carolina Panthers and quarterback Cam Newton are probably unbeatable.” NO PREDICTED SCORE GIVEN.
    DERRICK LEVASSEUR (Big Brother 16 winner): “The Carolina Panthers’ defense will be too much for Manning and the offense. They won’t be able to score enough points to keep up with Carolina’s offense. Cam Newton is playing lights out right now and if they rush him like they rushed Brady, he’s going to take off running. If they sit back, he’ll just pick them apart.” PANTHERS 28, BRONCOS 10.
    DORIS ROBERTS (actress, Everybody Loves Raymond): “Time to watch the guys in tight pants throw the football again! I’ll also be watching to see if my “Swipe For Doritos” spot wins the Doritos’ Crash The Super Bowl contest. If it does, it airs during the big game telecast. Cross your fingers! At game’s end, the Denver Broncos will be hoisting the Lombardi trophy.” BRONCOS 27, PANTHERS 24.
    MEREDITH VIEIRA (The Meredith Vieira Show): "I think the Denver Broncos will win, because who doesn't love Peyton Manning?" NO PREDICTED SCORE GIVEN.
    TAYLOR HICKS (American Idol Season 5 winner): “A running Cam Newton will neutralize the pass rush and send the Panthers to victory.” PANTHERS 28, BRONCOS 24.
    LILY THOMAS (my daughter and future pop singer): “I want to see Cam dab all game,” PANTHERS 28, BRONCOS 21.
    MELISSA GISONI (Lifetime’s Dance Moms): “I think the Denver Broncos will win the Super Bowl by a narrow margin under the experienced leadership of Peyton Manning.” BRONCOS 27, PANTHERS 24.
    HOLLY FRAZIER (Lifetime’s Dance Moms): “My gut (and my 17 year old son) tell me that the Super Bowl belongs to the Carolina Panthers this year. My prediction is based on Cam Newton playing like a NFL MVP and Carolina playing as a championship team the entire season.” NO PREDICTED SCORE GIVEN.

    GEORGE FOREMAN (Boxer): “I pick the Carolina Panthers. The Panthers score too many points. Pulling for Manning though.” PANTHERS 31, BRONCOS 24.
    JERRY MATHERS (actor, Leave it to Beaver): “In what I think may be Peyton Manning’s final game with the Broncos, he is definitely my sentimental favorite. However, Cam Newton and the Panthers are a powerful force and are multi-talented on both offense and defense. The Panthers win Super Bowl 50.” PANTHERS 20, BRONCOS 13.
    HISHAM TAWFIQ (actor, NBC’s The Blacklist): “I think the Panthers will win 24-17 ( I wouldn’t be surprised if Carolina put up 35 points). Cam and the Panthers are younger and the offense and defense are extremely dangerous. The Broncos defense is equally as dangerous, but I believe the offense is fading and will not be able to match Carolina’s.” PANTHERS 24, BRONCOS 17.
    BRANDON SPINK (actor): “I know that people are rooting for Peyton to win, but I feel the Panthers will win. One reason is the accurate throws by Cam Newton. He is one of the best quarterbacks in the league and is predicted to win the MVP honors. The second reason is the defense of the Panthers is unstoppable. – the linebackers are among the top in the league.” PANTHERS 31, BRONCOS 17.
    JENNIE FINCH-DAIGLE (Olympic softball pitcher): “Panthers are on a roll and are going to be tough to beat. However, I’m hoping Peyton is able to go out on top.” BRONCOS 23, PANTHERS 20.
    ROB PAULSEN (voice actor): “As much as I admire Peyton, his age not withstanding, I gotta go with Cam Newton and the Carolina Cats. It just feels like it’s their time. Amazing season, incredible quarterback (who runs like a halfback) and palpable momentum. Denver’s defense is crazy good and DEEP but I’ll go with Carolina.” PANTHERS 31, BRONCOS 20.”

A win for Manning and the Broncos would be an epic finish to a hall-of-fame career – something I think everyone is secretly rooting for. Unfortunately, he’ll be playing against the 15-1 Carolina Panthers (17-1 including playoffs), a team with one of the most elite defenses and a dual threat quarterback, Cam Newton, who seems to be unstoppable. This will be an instant classic, no doubt. As much as I’d like to see Number 18 go out with a bang, I have to put my money on Cam and the Panthers.” PANTHERS 28, BRONCOS 20.
    Page 7 of 7 - MIKE LOOKINLAND (actor, Brady Bunch and big Oakland Raiders fan): “For years, I’ve been rooting for both of those teams – to lose! Ha! Carolina will double Denver’s score.” PANTHERS 34, BRONCOS 17.
    CHRISTOPHER KNIGHT (actor, Brady Bunch): "Gotta go with the beasts from the east, the Carolina Panthers. After what the Panthers did to the Cardinals, on both sides of the ball, I'm unable to conceive of a positive outcome for Denver or a last stand by Payton Manning.
    "Cam Newton is like a human Indominus Rex. His size and strength are not completely unique at his position but his speed is. So, too, is his agility. He is flat-out intimidating. And now he has confidence. He's always had swagger, but now it's not a form of over-compensation-for-doubt-kind-of-swagger, but real, honest, earned "I got this" confidence. Containing him will be impossible even for the NFL's top-ranked defense. As big a problem as Cam is on offense, the same can be said of Carolina's D; and one feeds the other. Cam doesn't have to press knowing the points will be hard to come by for Peyton and crew. In fact, I predict Peyton will be concussed in the second quarter and due to the NFL's concussion protocol, will be the first star quarterback to be prevented from completing a Super Bowl. The controversy will be large. With all the money on the game, the pressure will be huge to let Peyton play but with the credibility of the NFL and its interest in player health, it's very future will be at stake and Peyton will not be allowed back in the game. The refs, the NFL, modern medicine will all be trending well into baseball season.
    "If this doesn't happen, then my premonition was of a film about Super Bowl and a QB concussion and all the drama that ensues." PANTHERS 31, BRONCOS 17.
