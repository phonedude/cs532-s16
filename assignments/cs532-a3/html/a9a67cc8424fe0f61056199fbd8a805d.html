NatWest Schools Cup 2015-16: Bromsgrove march on to under-18 quarter finals with Bedford Modern win

Holders Bromsgrove in dominant form – and they will need to keep their standards high with Royal Grammar School, High Wycombe, away next up

Champions: Luke White, the Bromsgrove captain, lifts the trophy at Twickenham Photo: SCHOOLSPORTSPHOTOGRAPHY.CO.UK

By Gareth A Davies

3:21PM GMT 06 Feb 2016

Follow

Comments

NatWest Schools Cup under-18 holders Bromsgrove put in an ominous eight-try display against Bedford Modern to reach the quarter finals as 48-8 victors yet head of rugby Tony Windo played down the success insisting the result was not a true reflection of the game.

Windo, the ex-Gloucester and Worcester Warriors prop forward, told Telegraph Sport: "The score line somewhat flattered Bromsgrove as they were made to work hard to overcome a very committed Bedford Modern team.

"Having not played together for almost two months Bromsgrove looked ring rusty in open play and made a number of errors in the opening exchanges. With a short turn around until the quarter final game against a very physical and well drilled Royal Grammar School High Wycombe side we will have to eradicate these errors if we are to stand any chance of progressing."

The victory over Bedford Modern was built around powerful forward momentum through an ebullient front five and back row in loose play. Loose-head prop Henry Walker carried well, James Taylor was commandeering at fly-half, and captain and centre Alexander Nisbet was also excellent. Bromsgrove built a powerful lead in the first half, turning around at 31-3.

Soon after the restart, Bedford fell further behind at 48-3. Yet Bedford's defence and pride is their performance saw them battle back to even up the second half. Right now, though, Bromsgrove have put down a marker, and they will have to match this performance against RGS High Wycombe, who also possess an outstanding team this season.

"We scraped a draw against RGS in this year's school fixture, and we know we will be facing our toughest test so far next week when we travel to there, for another away match, having been away in all rounds except Round 3,” Windo added.

That match will be played on Wednesday February 10. The winners could go on to be favourites to lift the trophy at Twickenham.
