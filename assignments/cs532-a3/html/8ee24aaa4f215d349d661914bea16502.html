Watch all the Super Bowl 50 ads that are online already

Alan Siegel, USA Today 9:30 AM. EST February 04, 2016

Wiener stampede (Photo: Heinz, YouTube, Custom)

CONNECT TWEET LINKEDIN GOOGLE+ PINTEREST

With Super Bowl 50 coming up fast, companies have begun to roll out their full ad campaigns. Here’s a list of all the commercials that have been released to the public so far. We’ll be updating this every day, so check back throughout the week.

Acura, “What He Said”

With the sounds of Van Halen’s “Runnin’ with the Devil” playing in the background, Acura introduces the new NSX.

Avocados From Mexico, “Avocados in Space”

While touring an outer-space museum filled with items from earth, a gaggle of aliens discover the beauty of the avocado.

Axe, “Find Your Magic”

AXE encourages men to embrace what makes them unique.

Budweiser, “Simply Put”

While eating a cheeseburger and drinking a Bud, Academy Award winner Helen Mirren decries drunk driving.

Colgate, “Save Water”

In this PSA, Colgate encourages America to save water by turning off the faucet.

Death Wish Coffee, “Storm’s a-Brewin'”

The winner of Intuit’s “Small Business, Big Game” contest, Death Wish Coffee offers a product that’s Viking strong.

Heinz, “Wiener Stampede”

A bunch of hot dogs (dachshunds to be exact) make a mad dash for people dressed up like bottles a variety of Heinz condiments.

Honda, “A New Truck to Love”

In this ad for Honda’s new Ridgeline pickup truck, sheep belt out Queen’s “Somebody to Love.”

Hyundai, “The Chase”

The new Hyundai Elantra’s remote start system saves two people who are being chased by a pair of talking bears.

Hyundai, “Ryanville”

While driving through a small town, two women are distracted by the fact that every single person they see is movie star Ryan Reynolds.

Kia, “Walken Closet” (extended cut)

When a man is in danger of blending in like beige socks, Christopher Walken encourages him to drive a Kia Optima, which the actor likens to “the world’s most exciting pair of socks.”

LG, “The Man From The Future”

Actor Liam Neeson stars as The Man From the Future in a spot for LG’s line of OLED TVs.

Mini, “Defy Labels”

Serena Williams, Abby Wambach, T-Pain, Randy Johnson, Tony Hawk, Harvey Keitel and more star in Mini’s campaign, which asks viewers to defy labels.

Pokémon, “Train On” (extended cut)

Chock full of references that fans of the wildly popular game series will love, Pokémon’s first Super Bowl ad celebrates franchise’s 20th anniversary.

Quicken Loans, “What We Were Thinking”

Quicken Loans touts its new Rocket Mortgage program, which it hopes will “turn an intimidating process into an easy one.”

Shock Top, “Unfiltered Talk” (extended cut)

Comedian T.J. Miller trades insults with Shock Top’s mascot: an anthropomorphic orange slice named Wedgehead.

Skittles, “Portrait”

Aerosmith lead singer Steven Tyler commissions a Skittles portrait, which ends up singing to him.

SoFi, “Great Loans for Great People”

Lender SoFi asks Americans if they have greatness in them.

SunTrust, “Hold Your Breath”

Actor Gary Sinise narrates SunTrust’s first Super Bowl ad, which encourages people to alleviate their financial stress.

T-Mobile, “Restricted Bling”

Drake’s “Hotline Bling” video shoot is interrupted by executives demanding that he alter the lyric “call me on my cellphone” to reflect rival wireless carriers’ expensive fees.
