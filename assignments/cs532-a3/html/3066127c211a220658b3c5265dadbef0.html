(CNN) - Some people, especially non-sports fans, only watch the Super Bowl for the commercials.

But should companies shell out big bucks for their products? That's the $5 million question - literally.

Ads for Super Bowl 50 cost a record-breaking $5 million and, for the first time ever, the same ads will be streamed online.

So, is it worth the price? Food and beverage giant PepsiCo says it's a no-brainer.

"The investment around this platform of Super Bowl is a big platform that, for fans of the NFL, is not just about that Sunday," Pepsi chief marketing officer Seth Kaufman said. "All the analysis we do is worth the money we're putting against it because we're getting great payback across our business."

The Super Bowl represents a large target market for the food and beverage industry, so it's important to create a marketing strategy surrounding the game at the point of purchase.

"We'll have 75,000 displays in every one of our retailers across the country," Ram Krishnan, chief marketing officer for Frito-Lay, said. "Two weeks leading up to the Super Bowl is the biggest purchase on snack and beverages. So, for sure, if you look at the entirety of it, it definitely pays off."

Super Bowl commercials need to be not only entertaining and provocative to be memorable, but also must not go too far, otherwise it risks alienating the customer.

"Especially with a brand like Doritos, which is targeted to a 19-year-old consumer, we're always going to get creative, which is going to offend someone," Krishnan said.

That tends to lead to safe, non-offensive themes like animals and babies.

"For five years, I think we've finished as the No. 1 spot. It's sense of humor. You've got to entertain," Krishnan said. I think as a brand you've got to entertain the consumers. Obviously kids and animals tend to do that."

Pepsi, however, has leaned on celebrity endorsements from Michael J. Fox and Cindy Crawford in the 1980s and 1990s to Britney Spears and even Elton John in more recent years.

"We used to hire celebrities as spokesperson," Krishnan said. "No longer the case. Now, we want to understand what's their narrative and what's the value they're adding? So, it's very different from how we've traditionally used celebrities in the past."

This year will be no different. Several commercials have been released in advance of the game. Animals and celebrities are among the highlights of those ads.

As for what else can be expected, you'll just have to wait until Sunday night.
