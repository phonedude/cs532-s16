Why the Broncos will pull off a shocker and win Super Bowl 50

By Dave Blezow

  • View author archive
  • email the author
  • follow on twitter
  • Get author RSS feed

February 4, 2016 | 11:05pm

Modal Trigger
Von Miller and the Broncos' fierce defense will give the Panthers fits, Dave Blezow writes. Photo: Getty Images

Going into the AFC Championship, the reasoning here was that because Peyton Manning lacked mobility and has displayed a rag arm at times this season, the Patriots’ defense would be able to load up to stop the run, jump the short pass routes and dictate where Manning could and couldn’t throw.

That’s not what happened. The Broncos were able to run the ball a lot (30 times) if not all that far (99 yards). That threat allowed Manning to throw on his schedule, and even if the passes weren’t pretty, two of them still ended up in Owen Daniels’ hands in the end zone and 20 points went up on the board, enough to win by two.

The real takeaway, though, from Broncos-Patriots was the Denver defense, the incessant pounding Tom Brady took (by the way, that guy is still incredible, leading that last touchdown drive basically looking through his ear hole on almost every play). It was also a sight to behold the hits the Denver secondary put on Danny Amendola and Julian Edelman, at times sending those small Patriots receivers three or four yards in the air after contact.

Speaking of takeaways, they are the lifeblood of this Carolina team. They were plus-20 in turnover ratio in the regular season, and that thievery has carried over to the playoffs, where they are plus-8 in two games. Luke Kuechly’s pick-six against Russell Wilson made it 14-0 before many fans had even reached their seats in the divisional round, helping the Panthers cruise to a 31-0 halftime lead before holding on for dear life, 31-24. Then in the NFC Championship, Carolina’s defense tortured Carson Palmer with four interceptions and two forced fumbles, creating seven turnovers in all, counting Patrick Peterson’s fumbled punt.

That 49-15 result (the Panthers hit the Over all by themselves) is what created a spread for the Super Bowl that’s almost double what it should be. Last week, Jay Kornegay, the vice president of race and sports operations at the Westgate Las Vegas Superbook, explained in The Post that if the results of the title games were disregarded, the Panthers would be three-point favorites over the Broncos on a neutral field.

He said the public, including people who make the Super Bowl their only bet of the year, would jump on the Panthers because of their NFC title domination, and at the end, the savvy bettors (”the sharps”) would come in and take the Broncos with the extra spread value. That’s exactly what’s happened with the line, which went from 3½ all the way to six, before coming back to 5½.

Two years ago, the Broncos made a mess of the New York/New Jersey Super Bowl. They fell behind the Seahawks 2-0 on the first snap, 22-0 by the half and 34-0 before they scored their first point.

Even a bad experience counts as experience, though, and the veterans of that game certainly are cognizant of the need to start strong and not get blown out of the building early by the Panthers.

Manning against the Carolina ball-hawking defense will be interesting, but the real show will be Cam Newton against the edge-rushing, hard-hitting Denver D. It’s been mostly a joyride for Newton thus far, but there have been a couple of moments of pause. In the Giants game in December, he limped off once and was clutching his shoulder on another occasion. He will put himself in positions to take some big shots, and if one of them causes real damage, the Cats are cooked.

This could matter, too: Manning has brother Eli in his ear, telling him how he threw four touchdown passes and put up 35 points on Carolina.

And finally, there are these facts: Eight of the last 14 Super Bowls have been decided by four points or fewer, and the underdog covered in six of the previous seven Super Bowls before last year’s, which was a pick ’em game and had no favorite/underdog.

The picks: Broncos +5½ and Under 44½

Broncos, 23-20

NFC/AFC title games: 1-1 vs. spread, 1-1 Over/Unders.
