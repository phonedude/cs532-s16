Why the Panthers will win Super Bowl 50

February 7, 2016 11:07am EST February 7, 2016 11:07am EST There are plenty of reasons to be optimistic the Panthers can win the franchise's first Super Bowl.
NFL
Share
Tweet
Email
By Ron Clements
Omnisport
@Ron_Clements
Published on Feb. 7, 2016

The Panthers opened as 3.5-point favorites to beat the Broncos in Super Bowl 50. As confidence in the Panthers grew, the line swelled to six points this week and settled at 5.5 points.

There are plenty of reasons to think the Panthers can win the franchise's first Super Bowl.

They have the NFL's MVP in quarterback Cam Newton, one of the most consistent running games in the NFL, an aggressive and opportunistic defense and a solid special teams unit.

As long as the Panthers continue to play the way they've performed all season, they should be hoisting the Lombardi Trophy come Sunday night at Levi's Stadium in Santa Clara, Calif.

Newton has the combination of size, speed, strength and savvy never before seen at the quarterback position. The 6-5, 245-pound Georgia native threw for 3,837 yards with 35 touchdowns and only 10 interceptions during the regular season while rushing for 636 yards and 10 scores. Newton can become the first quarterback ever to win a college football national championship, the Heisman Trophy, NFL MVP and a Super Bowl title. He would join Hall of Fame running back Marcus Allen as the only players at any position to achieve all four feats. Allen was also the MVP of Super Bowl XVIII and Newton would be a favorite to be the Super Bowl 50 MVP.

Newton's counterpart, 18-year veteran and five-time NFL MVP Peyton Manning, will have to contend with a Panthers defense that finished sixth in total defense and was fourth against the run. The Panthers were also sixth in sacks — the Broncos were first — and do a great job of collapsing the pocket because of defensive tackle Kawann Short, who led the team with 11 sacks. 

Behind Short are Pro Bowl linebackers Thomas Davis and Luke Kuechly, who was the 2013 NFL Defensive Player of the Year. Both Davis and Kuechly recorded more than 100 tackles for the fourth straight season. The Panthers also have depth at linebacker, winning all three games without Kuechly after he suffered a concussion in the regular-season opener.

MORE: Cam Newton quiz: Are you a lover or a hater? | How the Panthers were built, in 10 steps

Manning has thrown 17 interceptions in only 10 games this season, and the Panthers led the NFL by a large margin with a plus-20 turnover ratio. The Panthers had 24 interceptions during the regular season, led by safety Kurt Coleman's seven. Kuechly, Davis and shutdown corner Josh Norman had four picks apiece. Coleman and Kuechly have interceptions in the postseason, and Kuechly has returned half of his six total picks for touchdowns, including one in each playoff game. The Panthers have playmakers at each level, one of the reasons the Broncos may have difficulty moving the ball.

The Panthers jumped out to huge leads in wins over the Seahawks and Cardinals, but even if they fall behind, they might be the most resilient team in the NFL. Despite losing leading receiver Kelvin Benjamin in the training camp, 2014 sacks leader Charles Johnson for nine weeks and cornerbacks Charles Tillman and Bene Benwikere, the Panthers started the season 14-0 and finished with an NFL-best 15-1 record. 

They won't be rattled by any situation and have been loose all season as coach Ron Rivera has allowed his players to be true to their personalities. 

The Panthers also have the edge at running back with a healthy Jonathan Stewart to go with Newton to lead a ground attack that has rushed for at least 100 yards in 29 straight games. Carolina tight end Greg Olsen is one of the best in the NFL and led the Panthers with 77 receptions for 1,104 yards and seven scores. 

Maybe the two position groups most undervalued are the Panthers receivers and offensive line. Without Benjamin, other receivers stepped up with Corey Brown (31 catches, 447 yards, four touchdowns), rookie Devin Funchess (31-473-5), Jerricho Cotchery (39-485-3) and Ted Ginn (44-739-10) turning in solid seasons. Ginn is also a playmaker on special teams — another area where the Panthers have the edge.

Outside of wide receiver, the Panthers seem to have more talent at every position. That should translate to the arrival of the first Lombardi Trophy at Bank of America Stadium.
