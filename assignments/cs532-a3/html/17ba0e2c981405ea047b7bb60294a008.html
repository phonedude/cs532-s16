      Curtis Martin suggests Jets could have made Super Bowl

      Print Email
      Dom Cosentino | NJ Advance Media for NJ.com By Dom Cosentino | NJ Advance Media for NJ.com The Star-Ledger
      Email the author | Follow on Twitter
      on February 04, 2016 at 5:20 PM, updated February 04, 2016 at 10:18 PM
      comments

      The Jets' season-ending loss at the Bills infamously bounced them from making the playoffs. It was a gut punch for most fans—and even for owner Woody Johnson—not the least because this year's AFC was wide open.

      Jets owner Woody Johnson finally speaks Johnson on Friday met with reporters at a Jets fan event in Manhattan, for his first at-length public comments since March. What did he think of Year 1 under Todd Bowles? 1/29/2016 (Video by Darryl Slater | NJ Advance Media for NJ.com)

      (That the Broncos are the AFC's Super Bowl representative at a time when Peyton Manning is more or less Willie Mays-with-the-Metsing through games only underscores this reality.)

      Jets wideout Brandon Marshall this week told the New York Daily News he thought the Jets could have made this year's Super Bowl had they made the playoffs. And now Hall of Fame running back and ex-Jets great Curtis Martin has echoed that sentiment.

      "I think that [the Jets are] very close," Martin told Newsday at Super Bowl 50 in San Francisco. "Who knows what would have happened had they won that game? And as you can see with Denver playing the Patriots, everyone thought the Patriots would win, but any given Sunday can change that."

      MORE: What NFL coaches, scouts look for at Senior Bowl

      Martin, who played in Super Bowl XXXI as a member of the Patriots, also told Newsday it "would be the ultimate" for him to see the Jets in the Super Bowl. Curtis Martin is basically like any other guy who spends his Sundays wearing a Curtis Martin jersey.
