Key & Peele will comment live during Super Bowl, only they're not allowed to call it that

By Alyssa Pereira

Updated 5:42 pm, Monday, February 1, 2016
(Courtesy Terry Wei)
(Courtesy Terry Wei)

Keegan-Michael Key and Jordan Peele, comedians and stars of sketch show "Key & Peele," have had a long-running joke about the some of the sillier names of college and professional football players, and this Sunday, they'll take it a step further by giving live commentary on the Super Bowl.

There's just one problem: they're legally not allowed to use official terms like "Super Bowl" and apparently, "football."

As a result, Key and Peele, or rather, their characters Lee and Morris of totally-real-and-not-at-all-fake-TV-show "Real Talk," will be offering up their vaguely-worded reactions to the athletic actions during the sports game that is happening on Sunday in Santa Clara.

Tune into their live broadcast this Sunday to listen to their commentary.
