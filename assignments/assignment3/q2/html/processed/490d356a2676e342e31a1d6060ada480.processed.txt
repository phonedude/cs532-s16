 Last seen suffering the most humiliating death in Game of Thrones history, Charles Dance hints that his character, Tyrwin Lannister might not be gone for good. While promoting his upcoming film, Dracula Untold, Dance told MTV, “I’m not completely missing out on the next series. You haven’t seen the last of Tywin Lannister.”
Dance may have been Lord of Casterly Rock, Shield of Lannisport, Warden of the West and the scheming head of the powerful Lannister family but that didn’t stop him from being killed off in the bloody closing seconds of the fourth season of HBO’s famously bloody Game of Thrones on June 15, 2014.
This is big news for all Game Of Throne (GoT) fans, not only will they get to see more of the 67 year old English actor but also because it adds more weight to rumours that GoT is abandoning its no-flashbacks policy, maybe that means Sean Bean(Ned Stark) could also be rising from the dead as the series continues. The mythical tale of seven noble families fighting for control of the land of Westeros returns to the screen next April 2015.
