An Ontario County (N.Y.) Sheriff's Department update flooded the inboxes of news media covering the announcement Stewart was eligible to qualify for the Chase for the Sprint Cup.--Two weeks later, just minutes after interviews of the 16 championship contenders had launched at Chase Media Day in the nation's third-largest media market, the word broke that the investigation into Kevin Ward Jr.'s death had ended.--Two days after a thrilling Chase opener at Chicagoland Speedway, the news arrived that a grand jury would weigh the merits of taking action against Stewart, whose sprint car struck and killed Ward in an Aug. 9 race at Canandaigua Motorsports Park.Tony Stewart case will go to grand juryThe juxtaposition is jarring for the NASCAR industry and its followers because stock-car racing mostly has thrived through a culture of tightly controlled consistency for more than 65 years.Though it might qualify as the longest-running soap opera in professional sports, built on the emotionally charged and unpredictable calamity of driver feuds and fender banging, there is an insular constancy that also grounds NASCAR. Its weekly schedule hardly has varied in 40 years aside from the addition of a few tracks and the reshuffling of others.Its stars, who generally stay in the good graces of the law and lack unionization, rarely miss races, even in an inherently hazardous occupation. Gordon has started all 39 Cup races at New Hampshire Motor Speedway; he'll make it 40 Sunday. The four-time champion has 752 consecutive starts, and 10 more drivers have appeared in more than 300 straight races.It helps feed the comforting illusion of being impervious to any controlling forces outside the iron-fisted hubs of power in Daytona Beach, Fla., and Charlotte, N.C. — which might explain why many fans flooded social media this week in disbelief that Stewart was facing such an uncertain future.In this instance, real life keeps trickling in through the cracks, and the harshest reality is no one knows when the ground beneath the three-time champion's feet will stop shaking — or where he will land when it does.A raft of legal experts took to the airwaves and the Internet this week with varying predictions for the grand jury, ranging from the certitude of criminal charges to the likelihood of clearance.The truth is it's impossible to forecast what lies ahead short of being inside the sealed hearing where the evidence will be presented by district attorney Michael Tantillo.It's just as ambiguous what that evidence entails (no details have been provided about a second video that was obtained), or how it will be processed by 23 people in Upstate New York who will decide whether to return an indictment.If the result is unfavorable for Stewart, NASCAR — in the midst of promoting its ballyhooed and reformatted championship battle — will be faced with how to handle the negative headlines incurred by a three-time champion and superstar who is immensely popular but can be polarizing.In the wake of the NFL struggling with how to handle players facing criminal charges, the same questions will be bound to surround Stewart and shift focus away from the Chase.It'll be another disconcerting twist of a sport being superseded by real life.Follow Ryan on Twitter @nateryanCONNECTTWEETLINKEDINCOMMENTEMAILMORERead or Share this story: http://www.wcnc.com/story/sports/motor/nascar/2014/09/19/ryan-tony-stewart-saga-keeps-nascar-reeling/15893771/
